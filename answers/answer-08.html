Detailed Explanations

Reading Passage: "The Treasure Map" (Q1-5)

Haris found an old piece of paper in his grandfather's attic. It looked like a treasure map! There was an X marked near the old oak tree in the backyard.

"Grandpa, is this a real treasure map?" Haris asked excitedly.

Grandpa's eyes twinkled. "Only one way to find out."

Haris grabbed a small shovel and ran to the oak tree. He dug carefully where the X was marked. Soon, his shovel hit something hard. It was a metal box!

Inside the box were old photographs of Grandpa as a young boy, some old coins, and a note that said, "The real treasure is family and memories."

Haris hugged the box tightly. He understood now that some treasures are worth more than gold.

Q1 Where did Haris find the treasure map?
Correct Answer: C) In his grandfather's attic
The story says Haris "found an old piece of paper in his grandfather's attic."
Q2 Where was the X marked on the map?
Correct Answer: B) Near the old oak tree
The map had "an X marked near the old oak tree in the backyard."
Q3 What did Haris find inside the metal box?
Correct Answer: C) Old photographs, coins, and a note
Inside the box were "old photographs of Grandpa as a young boy, some old coins, and a note."
Q4 What did the note say?
Correct Answer: B) The real treasure is family and memories
The note said exactly this - "The real treasure is family and memories."
Q5 What is the main message of this story?
Correct Answer: B) Family and memories are more precious than gold
Haris "understood now that some treasures are worth more than gold."

Poem: "Books Are Magic" (Q6-8)

Open a book and step inside,
To worlds of wonder, far and wide.
Meet dragons brave and knights so bold,
Discover stories yet untold.

Fly through space or sail the sea,
A book can take you anywhere for free.
Turn the pages, let your mind soar,
With every book, you'll learn much more.

Q6 According to the poem, what can you meet in books?
Correct Answer: B) Dragons and knights
The poem says "Meet dragons brave and knights so bold."
Q7 What does the poem say books can do for free?
Correct Answer: A) Take you anywhere
"A book can take you anywhere for free."
Q8 Complete the poem with a word that rhymes: "Words on a page, a story to tell, With every chapter, you know it ___________."
Correct Answer: C) well
"Well" rhymes with "tell" in "Words on a page, a story to tell."

Language Skills (Q9-16)

Q9 Which picture best matches the setting? The passengers buckled their seatbelts as the pilot announced they would be landing soon. Through the window, they could see the city getting bigger and bigger.
Correct Answer: B) An airplane
Pilot, seatbelts, landing, and seeing city from above all indicate an airplane.
Q10 You want to persuade your friend to join the art club. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) In the art club, you'll learn amazing techniques and make cool projects!
This is persuasive - it highlights exciting benefits to convince someone to join.
Q11 Put the sentences in order: 1. Then, it floated up into the sky. 2. First, we bought a colorful balloon. 3. Finally, it disappeared among the clouds. 4. Unfortunately, the string slipped from my hand.
Correct Answer: A) 2, 4, 1, 3
First (2) bought balloon, Unfortunately (4) string slipped, Then (1) floated up, Finally (3) disappeared.
Q12 Read the paragraph and add the best topic sentence: ___. They build dams using sticks and mud. These dams create ponds where beavers can live safely. Beavers also have flat tails that help them swim.
Correct Answer: A) Beavers are hardworking animals with amazing skills.
This topic sentence introduces the paragraph about beavers' abilities.
Q13 What does "let the cat out of the bag" mean in: Ali let the cat out of the bag about the surprise party.
Correct Answer: B) Ali accidentally told the secret
"Let the cat out of the bag" means to reveal a secret accidentally.
Q14 The classroom was so noisy that nobody could hear anything. Mrs. Khan clapped her hands three times. Instantly, every student stopped talking and looked at her with attention. What does this tell us about Mrs. Khan?
Correct Answer: B) She knows how to get students' attention
Her clapping instantly made everyone stop and listen - she's experienced at managing a classroom.
Q15 Choose the sentence with the correct punctuation.
Correct Answer: C) I live in Lahore, Pakistan.
Proper nouns (place names) need capital letters: Lahore, Pakistan.
Q16 Which reason fits best? Playing board games is a great family activity. ___. For example, we all laugh together when someone makes a funny move.
Correct Answer: B) It brings everyone together and creates happy memories.
This reason explains why board games are great family activities - togetherness and memories.

Reading Passage: "Morning Music" (Q17-18)

Every morning, I wake up before the sun rises. I sit on the branch outside the window and begin to sing. My song wakes up the family inside the house.

The little girl always waves at me through the glass. Sometimes she leaves breadcrumbs on the windowsill for me. I love her for that.

Q17 Who is telling this story?
Correct Answer: C) A bird
The narrator sits on a branch, sings, and eats breadcrumbs - definitely a bird.
Q18 What clues tell you who the narrator is?
Correct Answer: A) The narrator sits on a branch and sings
These are the key clues - only birds sit on branches outside windows and sing.

Entertainment & Conversation (Q19-20)

Q19 You want to entertain someone with a joke about snow. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) What do snowmen eat for breakfast? Frosted Flakes!
A joke using wordplay - Frosted Flakes sounds like a cold, snowy breakfast for a snowman.
Q20 Choose the best reply: Mom: Did you finish your homework? Child: ___
Correct Answer: C) Yes, I finished it an hour ago!
This is a polite, direct answer to the question asked.
