Reading Passage: "The Brave Little Ant" (Q1-5)

There was a tiny ant named Andy who lived in a big anthill. One day, dark clouds covered the sky and it started to rain heavily. Water began to flood the anthill!

"We must move to higher ground!" Andy shouted. But the other ants were too scared to move. Andy knew he had to be brave. He led the way through the storm, helping the young ants and carrying food supplies.

After many hours, the ant colony reached a safe hill. The rain stopped and the sun came out. All the ants cheered for Andy. "You saved us!" they said. Andy smiled. He learned that even the smallest creature can do big things when they are brave.

Q1

What problem did the ant colony face?

AThey ran out of food
BTheir home was flooding
CThey got lost in the forest
DA bird attacked them
✓ B) Their home was flooding
Explanation

The story clearly states "it started to rain heavily. Water began to flood the anthill!" This was the main problem that Andy needed to solve.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Food shortage is not mentioned - they even carried food supplies during escape.
C)Getting lost in a forest is not mentioned in the story.
D)No bird attack is mentioned anywhere in the passage.
Q2

How did the other ants feel at first?

ABrave
BHappy
CScared
DExcited
✓ C) Scared
Explanation

The text explicitly states "the other ants were too scared to move." This shows their initial reaction to the flooding was fear.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Andy was brave, but the other ants were scared at first.
B)Their home was flooding - they wouldn't be happy!
D)Excited doesn't fit the dangerous situation they faced.
Q3

What did Andy do to help?

AHe hid in a corner
BHe led the ants to safety
CHe called for help
DHe went to sleep
✓ B) He led the ants to safety
Explanation

Andy "led the way through the storm, helping the young ants and carrying food supplies." He took action and guided everyone to a safe hill.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Andy was brave and took action - he didn't hide.
C)He acted himself rather than waiting for outside help.
D)He stayed awake and active to save his colony.
Q4

What happened after the rain stopped?

AThe ants went back home
BAndy was punished
CThe ants cheered for Andy
DEveryone was still scared
✓ C) The ants cheered for Andy
Explanation

"The rain stopped and the sun came out. All the ants cheered for Andy. 'You saved us!' they said." They celebrated Andy's bravery.

Why other options are wrong:
A)The story doesn't mention them returning - they reached a safe hill.
B)Andy was celebrated as a hero, not punished!
D)They were happy and grateful, not scared anymore.
Q5

What is the main message of this story?

AAnts are not important
BRain is always dangerous
CEven small creatures can be brave and helpful
DNever help others
✓ C) Even small creatures can be brave and helpful
Explanation

The story ends with Andy learning "that even the smallest creature can do big things when they are brave." This is the moral - size doesn't limit courage.

Why other options are wrong:
A)The opposite is true - the story shows ants ARE important.
B)Rain isn't always dangerous - that's not the main message.
D)The story shows the value of helping others!

Poem Section

Poem: "Stars at Night" (Q6-8)

When the sun goes down to sleep,
Stars come out with secrets to keep.
Twinkling high up in the sky,
Like diamonds winking from on high.

I count them one by one by one,
Until the night is almost done.
They guide the way for those who roam,
And light the path that leads back home.

Q6

When do the stars come out?

AIn the morning
BAt noon
CWhen the sun goes down
DNever
✓ C) When the sun goes down
Explanation

The first line says "When the sun goes down to sleep, Stars come out with secrets to keep." Stars appear at night when the sun sets.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Stars are not visible in the morning - too bright.
B)At noon the sun is brightest - can't see stars.
D)The poem clearly says stars DO come out at night.
Q7

What are the stars compared to in the poem?

AFlowers
BDiamonds
CBirds
DClouds
✓ B) Diamonds
Explanation

The poem says "Like diamonds winking from on high." This is a simile - comparing twinkling stars to sparkling diamonds.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Flowers are not mentioned in the poem.
C)Birds are not mentioned anywhere.
D)Clouds are not mentioned in the poem.
Q8

Complete the poem with a word that rhymes:
"The moon shines bright throughout the night, The stars around it are a beautiful ___________."

Aday
Bsight
Csun
Dcloud
✓ B) sight
Explanation

"Night" and "sight" rhyme because they both end with the "-ight" sound. "A beautiful sight" also makes perfect sense - stars are lovely to look at!

Why other options are wrong:
A)"Day" doesn't rhyme with "night".
C)"Sun" doesn't rhyme with "night".
D)"Cloud" doesn't rhyme with "night".

Language Skills Section

Q9

Which book should you choose if you want to learn how to make paper airplanes?

A"The Adventures of Captain Zoom" (A story about space travel)
B"My First Pet Dog" (A book about caring for dogs)
C"Fun Paper Crafts for Kids" (A book with craft instructions)
D"Rainy Day Poems" (A collection of poems)
✓ C) "Fun Paper Crafts for Kids"
Explanation

Paper airplanes are paper crafts! A book with "craft instructions" would teach you how to fold and make paper airplanes step by step.

Why other options are wrong:
A)A story book about space - doesn't teach how to make things.
B)About dogs - not related to paper crafts.
D)Poetry collection - no craft instructions.
Q10

You want to persuade your parents to get a pet fish. What should you write?

AFish live in water and have fins.
BFish are easy to take care of and watching them swim is very relaxing!
CFish cannot walk or run.
DPet stores sell many types of fish.
✓ B) Fish are easy to take care of and watching them swim is very relaxing!
Explanation

Persuasive writing needs benefits and reasons. This option gives TWO good reasons: "easy to take care of" and "very relaxing" - making parents want to say yes!

Why other options are wrong:
A)Just facts about fish - doesn't explain why to get one.
C)Not a reason to get a fish - just a limitation.
D)Information about stores - not why fish are good pets.
Q11

Put the following sentences in order from beginning to end.
1. Then, she put bread in the toaster.
2. Finally, she enjoyed her breakfast.
3. First, Hina woke up early.
4. After that, she spread butter on the toast.

A3, 1, 4, 2
B1, 2, 3, 4
C3, 4, 1, 2
D2, 3, 4, 1
✓ A) 3, 1, 4, 2
Explanation

Follow the sequence words: "First" (wake up - 3), "Then" (bread in toaster - 1), "After that" (spread butter - 4), "Finally" (enjoy - 2). Order: 3, 1, 4, 2.

Why other options are wrong:
B)Can't put bread in toaster first - need to wake up!
C)Must toast bread before spreading butter.
D)Can't enjoy breakfast before making it.
Q12

Read the following paragraph. Then, add the best topic sentence.
"_______________________. Dogs can learn to sit, stay, and fetch. They can also be trained to help people who cannot see. Some dogs even help the police find missing people."

ACats are independent animals.
BDogs are very smart and can be trained to do many things.
CAll animals need food and water.
DFish make good pets.
✓ B) Dogs are very smart and can be trained to do many things.
Explanation

A topic sentence introduces the main idea. This paragraph is all about dogs being trained (sit, stay, fetch, helping blind people, police work). The topic sentence should mention dogs and training.

Why other options are wrong:
A)The paragraph is about dogs, not cats.
C)Too general - doesn't match the specific topic.
D)About fish - wrong animal entirely.
Q13

What does "piece of cake" mean in this sentence?
"The math test was a piece of cake for Ali."

AAli ate cake during the test
BThe test was very easy for Ali
CThe test was about cakes
DAli brought cake to school
✓ B) The test was very easy for Ali
Explanation

"Piece of cake" is an idiom (expression) meaning something is very easy to do. Just like eating cake is easy, the test was easy for Ali!

Why other options are wrong:
A)It's an idiom - not about actual cake eating.
C)Not literal - doesn't mean the test topic was cakes.
D)Not about bringing food - it's a figure of speech.
Q14

Read the passage and answer the question.
"Whiskers the cat loved quiet afternoons on the sofa. But today was different. The doorbell rang, and a new puppy came bounding in! Whiskers hissed at first, but soon they became best friends."

What is the main message of this passage?

ACats don't like sofas
BDoorbells are too loud
CNew friendships can start unexpectedly
DPuppies are always naughty
✓ C) New friendships can start unexpectedly
Explanation

Whiskers didn't expect a puppy and "hissed at first" but "soon they became best friends." The message is that friendships can bloom from unexpected beginnings.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Whiskers LOVED the sofa - opposite of this!
B)The doorbell isn't the main focus of the story.
D)The puppy became a best friend - not just naughty.
Q15

Choose the sentence with the correct punctuation.

Aahmed and sara went to the park.
BAhmed and Sara went to the park.
Cahmed And sara Went To The park.
DAhmed And Sara Went To The Park.
✓ B) Ahmed and Sara went to the park.
Explanation

Rules: Names (proper nouns) need capital letters (Ahmed, Sara). Regular words like "and," "went," "to," "the," "park" stay lowercase. Period at the end.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Names "ahmed" and "sara" need capital letters.
C)Random capitalization - inconsistent and wrong.
D)Too many capitals - only names should be capitalized.
Q16

Which reason fits best with the opinion and example?
"The park is the best place to spend a sunny day. _______________________. For example, you can play on the swings and slides."

AParks have grass and trees.
BParks are open every day.
CThere are many fun activities to do at the park.
DSome parks have parking lots.
✓ C) There are many fun activities to do at the park.
Explanation

The example mentions "swings and slides" - these are fun activities! The reason should connect the opinion (park is best) with the example (swings/slides). "Fun activities" does this perfectly.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Grass and trees aren't related to swings and slides.
B)Opening hours don't explain why it's the BEST place.
D)Parking lots aren't related to having fun.

Reading Comprehension - Part 2

Reading: "My Best Friend" (Q17-18)

I wag my tail whenever I see my human coming home. I love to play fetch in the garden and take long walks. My favorite treat is a crunchy biscuit. At night, I curl up at the foot of the bed and guard my family while they sleep.

Q17

Who is telling this story?

AA cat
BA child
CA dog
DA parent
✓ C) A dog
Explanation

The clues are: "wag my tail" (dogs wag tails), "play fetch" (a dog game), "my human" (dogs call owners "human"), "guard my family" (dogs guard). All point to a dog!

Why other options are wrong:
A)Cats don't typically wag tails or play fetch.
B)Children don't wag tails or call parents "my human."
D)Parents don't wag tails or guard at the foot of beds.
Q18

How does the narrator feel about their family?

AScared of them
BLoves and protects them
CDoesn't care about them
DAngry at them
✓ B) Loves and protects them
Explanation

The dog "wag[s] tail" when human comes home (showing love), "guard[s] my family while they sleep" (showing protection). The narrator clearly loves and protects the family.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Wagging tail and playing show happiness, not fear.
C)Guarding the family shows deep care.
D)Wagging tail shows joy, not anger.

Entertainment & Conversation

Q19

You want to entertain someone with a joke about school. What should you write?

ASchool starts at 8 AM every day.
BWhy did the student eat his homework? Because his teacher said it was a piece of cake!
CSchools have classrooms and libraries.
DStudents learn many subjects at school.
✓ B) Why did the student eat his homework? Because his teacher said it was a piece of cake!
Explanation

This is a joke with a pun! "Piece of cake" means easy, but the student takes it literally and EATS it. The wordplay between the idiom and actual eating makes it funny!

Why other options are wrong:
A)Just a fact about timing - not funny.
C)Information about buildings - not entertaining.
D)Educational information - not a joke.
Q20

Choose the best reply to complete the conversation.
Teacher: Can you help me carry these books?
Student: ___________________

ANo, I'm busy playing.
BBooks are heavy.
COf course! I'd be happy to help.
DI don't like books.
✓ C) Of course! I'd be happy to help.
Explanation

This is the polite, helpful, and respectful answer. When a teacher asks for help, saying "Of course! I'd be happy to help" shows good manners and willingness to assist.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Impolite - should help teachers, not refuse.
B)Doesn't answer the question - just a comment.
D)Rude and doesn't help the teacher.
