Reading Passage: "The Brave Little Ant"

Q1 What problem did the ant colony face?
A) They ran out of food - Wrong: Food shortage not mentioned
B) Their home was flooding - CORRECT: "Water began to flood the anthill!"
C) They got lost in the forest - Wrong: Not mentioned
D) A bird attacked them - Wrong: Not mentioned
Explanation: The story clearly states "it started to rain heavily. Water began to flood the anthill!" This was the main problem.
Q2 How did the other ants feel at first?
A) Brave - Wrong: Andy was brave, others were not
B) Happy - Wrong: They were in danger
C) Scared - CORRECT: "the other ants were too scared to move"
D) Excited - Wrong: They were frightened
Explanation: The text explicitly states "the other ants were too scared to move."
Q3 What did Andy do to help?
A) He hid in a corner - Wrong: He was brave, not hiding
B) He led the ants to safety - CORRECT: "He led the way through the storm"
C) He called for help - Wrong: He acted himself
D) He went to sleep - Wrong: He took action
Explanation: Andy "led the way through the storm, helping the young ants and carrying food supplies."
Q4 What happened after the rain stopped?
A) The ants went back home - Wrong: They stayed on the safe hill
B) Andy was punished - Wrong: He was celebrated
C) The ants cheered for Andy - CORRECT: "All the ants cheered for Andy"
D) Everyone was still scared - Wrong: They were happy and grateful
Explanation: "The rain stopped and the sun came out. All the ants cheered for Andy. 'You saved us!' they said."
Q5 What is the main message of this story?
A) Ants are not important - Wrong: Opposite of the message
B) Rain is always dangerous - Wrong: Not the main point
C) Even small creatures can be brave and helpful - CORRECT: The moral of the story
D) Never help others - Wrong: The opposite message
Explanation: The story ends with Andy learning "that even the smallest creature can do big things when they are brave."

Poem: "Stars at Night"

Q6 When do the stars come out?
A) In the morning - Wrong: Stars appear at night
B) At noon - Wrong: Too bright for stars
C) When the sun goes down - CORRECT: "When the sun goes down to sleep, Stars come out"
D) Never - Wrong: Stars do come out
Q7 What are the stars compared to in the poem?
A) Flowers - Wrong: Not mentioned
B) Diamonds - CORRECT: "Like diamonds winking from on high"
C) Birds - Wrong: Not mentioned
D) Clouds - Wrong: Not mentioned
Q8 Complete the poem with a rhyme for "night".
A) day - Wrong: Does not rhyme with "night"
B) sight - CORRECT: Rhymes with "night" and fits the meaning
C) sun - Wrong: Does not rhyme
D) cloud - Wrong: Does not rhyme

Language Skills

Q9 Which book for learning to make paper airplanes?
A) "The Adventures of Captain Zoom" - Wrong: A story book, not craft instructions
B) "My First Pet Dog" - Wrong: About pets
C) "Fun Paper Crafts for Kids" - CORRECT: Craft instructions would include paper airplanes
D) "Rainy Day Poems" - Wrong: Poetry, not crafts
Q10 Persuade your parents to get a pet fish.
A) Fish live in water and have fins - Wrong: Just facts, not persuasive
B) Fish are easy to take care of and watching them swim is very relaxing! - CORRECT: Gives benefits to persuade
C) Fish cannot walk or run - Wrong: Not a reason to get one
D) Pet stores sell many types of fish - Wrong: Just information
Q11 Order the sentences (getting ready).
A) 3, 1, 4, 2 - CORRECT: Wake up, bread in toaster, spread butter, enjoy
B) 1, 2, 3, 4 - Wrong: Waking up should be first
C) 3, 4, 1, 2 - Wrong: Bread must be toasted before butter
D) 2, 3, 4, 1 - Wrong: Cannot enjoy before making
Q12 Best topic sentence about dogs.
A) Cats are independent animals - Wrong: Paragraph is about dogs
B) Dogs are very smart and can be trained to do many things. - CORRECT: Matches the examples given
C) All animals need food and water - Wrong: Too general
D) Fish make good pets - Wrong: Wrong animal
Q13 What does "piece of cake" mean?
A) Ali ate cake during the test - Wrong: It is an idiom
B) The test was very easy for Ali - CORRECT: "Piece of cake" means something easy
C) The test was about cakes - Wrong: Not literal
D) Ali brought cake to school - Wrong: Not literal
Q14 Main message of Whiskers passage.
A) Cats don't like sofas - Wrong: Not the main point
B) Doorbells are too loud - Wrong: Not the message
C) New friendships can start unexpectedly - CORRECT: Cat and puppy became friends
D) Puppies are always naughty - Wrong: Not stated
Q15 Correct punctuation.
A) ahmed and sara went to the park. - Wrong: Names need capitals
B) Ahmed and Sara went to the park. - CORRECT: Proper nouns capitalized, period at end
C) ahmed And sara Went To The park. - Wrong: Incorrect capitalization
D) Ahmed And Sara Went To The Park. - Wrong: Too many capitals
Q16 Best reason for "park is best place on sunny day".
A) Parks have grass and trees - Wrong: Description, not reason for fun
B) Parks are open every day - Wrong: Availability, not why it is best
C) There are many fun activities to do at the park. - CORRECT: Matches example of swings/slides
D) Some parks have parking lots - Wrong: Irrelevant

Reading: "My Best Friend"

Q17 Who is telling this story?
A) A cat - Wrong: Cats do not wag tails typically
B) A child - Wrong: Narrator waits for "human"
C) A dog - CORRECT: Wags tail, plays fetch, guards family
D) A parent - Wrong: Dogs guard, not human behavior described
Q18 How does the narrator feel about their family?
A) Scared of them - Wrong: Loves and guards them
B) Loves and protects them - CORRECT: "guard my family while they sleep"
C) Doesn't care - Wrong: Shows love
D) Angry at them - Wrong: Shows affection
Q19 Entertain with a joke about school.
A) School starts at 8 AM - Wrong: Information, not a joke
B) Why did the student eat his homework? Because his teacher said it was a piece of cake! - CORRECT: A pun/joke
C) Schools have classrooms - Wrong: Just facts
D) Students learn many subjects - Wrong: Informational
Q20 Best reply to "Can you help me carry these books?"
A) No, I'm busy playing - Wrong: Impolite
B) Books are heavy - Wrong: Does not answer the question
C) Of course! I'd be happy to help. - CORRECT: Polite and helpful
D) I don't like books - Wrong: Rude and unhelpful
