Detailed Explanations

Reading Passage: "The Science Fair" (Q1-5)

Sana spent weeks working on her science fair project. She wanted to find out which type of music helped plants grow better - classical, rock, or no music at all.

She planted three identical seeds in identical pots. One listened to classical music, one listened to rock music, and one had no music. Every day, she measured their growth and recorded the data.

On the day of the science fair, Sana presented her findings. The plant with classical music grew the tallest! The judges were impressed with her careful research.

"First place goes to Sana!" the announcer said. Sana beamed with pride. All her hard work had paid off.

Q1 What was Sana's science project about?
Correct Answer: B) How music affects plant growth
Sana tested classical, rock, and no music on plants.
Q2 How many plants did Sana use in her experiment?
Correct Answer: C) Three
"She planted three identical seeds in identical pots."
Q3 What did Sana do every day?
Correct Answer: B) Measured the plants and recorded data
"Every day, she measured their growth and recorded the data."
Q4 Which plant grew the tallest?
Correct Answer: B) The one with classical music
"The plant with classical music grew the tallest!"
Q5 What place did Sana win?
Correct Answer: C) First place
"First place goes to Sana!"

Poem: "The Little Seed" (Q6-8)

I am a little seed, so small and round,
Buried in the dark, cold ground.
But do not worry, do not fear,
Spring is coming, spring is near!

The rain will water, the sun will warm,
I will be safe from any storm.
And one fine day, I will break free,
And grow into a beautiful tree!

Q6 Where is the little seed?
Correct Answer: C) Buried in the ground
"Buried in the dark, cold ground."
Q7 What season is the seed waiting for?
Correct Answer: D) Spring
"Spring is coming, spring is near!"
Q8 Complete the poem with a word that rhymes: "My leaves will grow so green and tall, I will be the prettiest of ___________."
Correct Answer: B) all
"All" rhymes with "tall."

Language Skills (Q9-16)

Q9 Which picture best matches the setting? "The nurse took my temperature while Mom held my hand. Posters about health covered the walls of the small room. The doctor would come in soon."
Correct Answer: A) A doctor's office
Nurse, temperature, health posters, doctor = medical setting.
Q10 You want to persuade your mom to take you to the zoo. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) Can we please go to the zoo? I want to see the elephants and learn about different animals!
Persuasive with reasons (see elephants, learn about animals).
Q11 Put the sentences in order: 1. Then, I put on my costume. 2. Finally, I went trick-or-treating with my friends. 3. First, I decided to be a pirate for Halloween. 4. Next, I got my candy bucket ready.
Correct Answer: A) 3, 1, 4, 2
Halloween: decided costume (3), put on costume (1), got bucket (4), trick-or-treating (2).
Q12 Add the best topic sentence: "_______. They come in many shapes and sizes. Some kites look like dragons or butterflies. You need wind to fly a kite properly."
Correct Answer: B) Kites are fun toys that fly in the sky.
Topic sentence about kites matches the paragraph.
Q13 What does "break the ice" mean? "The new student told a joke to break the ice on his first day."
Correct Answer: B) Make people feel more comfortable
"Break the ice" = ease social tension.
Q14 Why were people crying at the end of the movie? "When the movie ended, almost everyone in the theater was wiping their eyes. The story about the little dog finding his way home had touched everyone's heart."
Correct Answer: B) The movie was touching and emotional
"The story...had touched everyone's heart."
Q15 Choose the sentence with the correct punctuation.
Correct Answer: C) Look at that beautiful sunset!
Exclamatory sentence needs exclamation mark.
Q16 Which reason fits best? "Team sports are important for children. _______. For example, playing soccer taught me how to work with others to reach a goal."
Correct Answer: B) They teach cooperation and teamwork.
Connects to example of working with others in soccer.

Reading Passage: "Building the Web" (Q17-18)

I work all night long, spinning silk from my body. Round and round I go, creating my beautiful trap. The moonlight makes my web sparkle like diamonds.

By morning, my web is complete. Now I wait patiently in the corner. A fly will come soon, and I will have breakfast. Spinning webs is hard work, but I am very good at it!

Q17 Who is telling this story?
Correct Answer: B) A spider
Spins silk, makes web, catches flies = spider.
Q18 What clues tell you who the narrator is?
Correct Answer: A) The narrator spins silk, makes a web, and catches flies
Classic spider characteristics.

Entertainment & Conversation (Q19-20)

Q19 You want to entertain someone with a joke about the sun. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) Why did the sun go to school? To get a little brighter!
A pun on "brighter" (smarter and more luminous).
Q20 Choose the best reply: Grandma: "I brought you a present!" Child: ___
Correct Answer: B) Thank you so much, Grandma! You are the best!
Grateful and loving response.
