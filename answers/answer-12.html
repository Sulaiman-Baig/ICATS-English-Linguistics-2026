Detailed Explanations

Reading Passage: "The Little Chef" (Q1-5)

Seven-year-old Amir loved watching cooking shows on TV. One day, he decided to make breakfast for his family.

He woke up early and tiptoed to the kitchen. First, he got out the bread. Then, he carefully cracked eggs into a bowl (only dropping one shell in by accident). He asked his older sister to help with the stove.

Together, they made scrambled eggs and toast. Amir also poured orange juice into glasses. He arranged everything on a tray with a flower from the garden.

When Mom and Dad came to the table, they were amazed. "Did you make all this?" Mom asked.

Amir nodded proudly. "With a little help from Sis!"

Q1 What inspired Amir to cook?
Correct Answer: B) Watching cooking shows on TV
"Amir loved watching cooking shows on TV."
Q2 What went wrong when Amir cracked the eggs?
Correct Answer: B) Some shell fell into the bowl
"Only dropping one shell in by accident."
Q3 Why did Amir need his sister's help?
Correct Answer: C) To use the stove safely
"He asked his older sister to help with the stove."
Q4 What special touch did Amir add to the tray?
Correct Answer: C) A flower from the garden
"He arranged everything on a tray with a flower from the garden."
Q5 How did Amir feel when he served breakfast?
Correct Answer: C) Proud
"Amir nodded proudly."

Poem: "The Kite" (Q6-8)

Up, up, up goes my kite so high,
A colorful dot in the bright blue sky.
The wind is strong, it pulls the string,
I hold on tight to this wonderful thing.

It dips and dives, it loops and sways,
I could watch it fly for days and days.
When the wind dies down, it comes back to me,
The happiest flyer you will ever see!

Q6 What makes the kite fly high?
Correct Answer: B) The wind
"The wind is strong, it pulls the string."
Q7 What does the speaker do when the wind pulls the string?
Correct Answer: B) Holds on tight
"I hold on tight to this wonderful thing."
Q8 Complete the poem with a word that rhymes: "My kite dances in the air so light, What a wonderful, beautiful ___________."
Correct Answer: C) sight
"Sight" rhymes with "light."

Language Skills (Q9-16)

Q9 Which picture best matches the setting? Animals of all kinds were in cages and enclosures. Children pointed at the monkeys swinging from branches. A zookeeper was feeding the penguins nearby.
Correct Answer: C) A zoo
Animals in cages, monkeys, zookeeper feeding penguins = zoo.
Q10 You want to persuade your parents to let you have a sleepover. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) A sleepover would be so much fun, and I promise we will be quiet and go to bed on time!
Persuasive - gives benefits and addresses parents' concerns.
Q11 Put the sentences in order: 1. Then, I put on my pajamas. 2. Finally, I went to sleep. 3. First, I took a warm bath. 4. Next, I brushed my teeth.
Correct Answer: A) 3, 1, 4, 2
Bedtime: First (3) bath, Then (1) pajamas, Next (4) brush teeth, Finally (2) sleep.
Q12 Read the paragraph and add the best topic sentence: ___. They have a hard shell that protects their body. When they feel scared, they pull their head and legs inside the shell. Turtles move very slowly on land but can swim faster in water.
Correct Answer: A) Turtles have special bodies that keep them safe.
Topic sentence about turtle protection matches the paragraph.
Q13 What does "keep your chin up" mean in: Even though he lost the game, the coach told him to keep his chin up.
Correct Answer: B) Stay positive and do not be sad
"Keep your chin up" means remain hopeful and positive.
Q14 Little Maryam had been playing all day at her cousin's birthday party. By evening, her eyes were drooping, and she could barely keep them open. Before anyone noticed, she was fast asleep on the couch. What is this passage mainly about?
Correct Answer: B) A tired child falling asleep after playing
Main idea: child played all day, got tired, fell asleep.
Q15 Choose the sentence with the correct punctuation.
Correct Answer: B) I have two sisters named Aisha and Maria.
Proper nouns (names) capitalized, correct punctuation.
Q16 Which reason fits best? Having a pet teaches responsibility. ___. For example, I feed my fish every morning and clean the tank every week.
Correct Answer: B) You have to take care of another living creature.
Explains why pets teach responsibility.

Reading Passage: "A Quiet Afternoon" (Q17-18)

My whiskers twitched as I heard a sound. I opened one eye lazily. Just the wind. I stretched my furry body and curled back into a ball on my favorite cushion.

The sun coming through the window made the perfect warm spot. Napping is what I do best, after all. Maybe later I will chase that red dot that my human likes to make appear on the floor.

Q17 Who is telling this story?
Correct Answer: C) A cat
Whiskers, furry body, napping, chasing red dot (laser pointer) = cat.
Q18 What clues tell you who the narrator is?
Correct Answer: A) The narrator has whiskers, a furry body, naps, and chases a red dot
All these are cat characteristics.

Entertainment & Conversation (Q19-20)

Q19 You want to entertain someone with a joke about birthdays. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) Why do candles go on top of birthday cakes? Because it is hard to put them on the bottom!
A funny, logical joke about birthday cakes.
Q20 Choose the best reply: New classmate: Hi, I am new here. What is your name? You: ___
Correct Answer: B) Hi! I am [name]. Welcome to our class! Want me to show you around?
Friendly, welcoming response to a new classmate.
