Reading Passage: "The Clever Crow" (Q1-5)

A thirsty crow was flying over a garden when she spotted a pitcher. She flew down excitedly, but when she looked inside, the water level was too low for her beak to reach.

The crow thought and thought. Then she had an idea! She picked up a small pebble with her beak and dropped it into the pitcher. The water rose a tiny bit. She dropped another pebble, then another, and another.

Slowly, the water level rose higher and higher. Finally, it was high enough for the crow to drink. She drank happily and flew away.

"Where there's a will, there's a way," she thought.

Q1

What problem did the crow face?

AShe was lost
BThe water was too low to reach
CThe pitcher was empty
DShe was too tired to fly
✓ B) The water was too low to reach
Explanation

The crow could not drink because "the water level was too low for her beak to reach."

Why other options are wrong:
A)She wasn't lost - she found the pitcher.
C)The pitcher had water, just too low.
D)She was thirsty, not tired.
Q2

How did the crow solve her problem?

AShe broke the pitcher
BShe asked for help
CShe dropped pebbles to raise the water
DShe found another pitcher
✓ C) She dropped pebbles to raise the water
Explanation

"She picked up a small pebble... and dropped it into the pitcher. The water rose."

Why other options are wrong:
A)She didn't break anything.
B)She solved it by herself.
D)She used the same pitcher.
Q3

What does "Where there's a will, there's a way" mean?

AYou should always give up
BIf you really want something, you'll find a way
CCrows are smarter than other birds
DWater is important
✓ B) If you really want something, you'll find a way
Explanation

This saying means determination leads to success - the crow wanted water and found a way!

Why other options are wrong:
A)Opposite meaning - it's about NOT giving up.
C)Not about comparing birds.
D)Not the meaning of the saying.
Q4

What word best describes the crow?

ALazy
BClever
CAngry
DScared
✓ B) Clever
Explanation

The crow is clever - she thought of a creative solution. The title is "The Clever Crow"!

Why other options are wrong:
A)She worked hard dropping pebbles - not lazy.
C)She wasn't angry, just thirsty.
D)She was determined, not scared.
Q5

What can we learn from this story?

ACrows cannot drink water
BPebbles are useless
CThinking carefully can solve problems
DPitchers are dangerous
✓ C) Thinking carefully can solve problems
Explanation

The main lesson: "The crow thought and thought. Then she had an idea!" - thinking leads to solutions.

Why other options are wrong:
A)The crow did drink water - opposite message.
B)Pebbles helped solve the problem!
D)The pitcher was helpful, not dangerous.

Poem: "Colors Everywhere" (Q6-8)

Red are the apples hanging on the tree,
Orange are the pumpkins for you and me.
Yellow is the sun that shines so bright,
Green is the grass that's a lovely sight.

Blue is the ocean so deep and wide,
Purple are the flowers growing outside.
Colors everywhere, what a beautiful view,
Each and every shade so wonderful and new.

Q6

According to the poem, what color is the sun?

ARed
BOrange
CYellow
DBlue
✓ C) Yellow
Explanation

"Yellow is the sun that shines so bright" - the poem says the sun is yellow.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Red is for apples in the poem.
B)Orange is for pumpkins.
D)Blue is for the ocean.
Q7

What color are the flowers in the poem?

ARed
BYellow
CGreen
DPurple
✓ D) Purple
Explanation

"Purple are the flowers growing outside" - the poem says flowers are purple.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Red is for apples.
B)Yellow is for the sun.
C)Green is for grass.
Q8

Complete with a rhyme: "The rainbow arcs across the sky, A colorful bridge way up so ___________."

Alow
Bhigh
Cfar
Dbig
✓ B) high
Explanation

"High" rhymes with "sky" - both end with the "-y" sound.

Why other options are wrong:
A)"Low" doesn't rhyme with "sky".
C)"Far" doesn't rhyme with "sky".
D)"Big" doesn't rhyme with "sky".

Language Skills Section

Q9

Which setting: "Doctor Amna put on her white coat and checked her list of patients..."?

AA school
BA hospital
CA restaurant
DA park
✓ B) A hospital
Explanation

Doctor, white coat, patients, hospital - all these clues point to a hospital setting.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Schools have teachers, not doctors with patients.
C)Restaurants have chefs, not doctors.
D)Parks don't have doctors checking patients.
Q10

To persuade your class to recycle more, what should you write?

ARecycling bins are green.
BRecycling helps protect our planet and saves animals' homes!
CPaper comes from trees.
DSome countries recycle more than others.
✓ B) Recycling helps protect our planet and saves animals' homes!
Explanation

Persuasive writing uses emotional appeal and benefits to convince people. This option shows why recycling matters.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Just a fact about bins - not persuasive.
C)Just a fact - doesn't persuade.
D)Just a comparison - not convincing.
Q11

Order: 1. Then, boarded airplane. 2. First, packed suitcases. 3. Finally, arrived. 4. Next, drove to airport.

A2, 4, 1, 3
B1, 2, 3, 4
C2, 1, 4, 3
D4, 2, 1, 3
✓ A) 2, 4, 1, 3
Explanation

First (2), Next (4), Then (1), Finally (3) - logical travel sequence.

Why other options are wrong:
B)"First" should come first, not "Then".
C)Must drive to airport before boarding.
D)"First" should come before "Next".
Q12

Best topic sentence for penguin paragraph?

ABirds can fly very high.
BPenguins are unique birds with interesting behaviors.
CFish live in the ocean.
DSwimming is fun.
✓ B) Penguins are unique birds with interesting behaviors.
Explanation

A topic sentence introduces the main idea. The paragraph is about penguins' unique behaviors.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Penguins can't fly - contradicts the paragraph.
C)The paragraph is about penguins, not fish.
D)Too general - doesn't introduce penguins.
Q13

What does "break a leg" mean before a school play?

ABe careful not to fall
BGood luck
CRun fast on stage
DHurt yourself
✓ B) Good luck
Explanation

"Break a leg" is a theater idiom meaning "good luck." It's NOT literal!

Why other options are wrong:
A)It's an idiom - not literal advice about falling.
C)Not about running on stage.
D)Never literally means to hurt yourself!
Q14

What does the passage tell us about the mother who sings a lullaby?

AShe is tired and angry
BShe is caring and gentle
CShe is busy and rushed
DShe does not like singing
✓ B) She is caring and gentle
Explanation

"Picked her up gently" and "sang a soft lullaby" show she is caring and gentle.

Why other options are wrong:
A)She acted gently - not angry.
C)She took time to comfort the baby.
D)She sang a lullaby - she does sing!
Q15

Choose the sentence with correct punctuation.

Amy favorite colors are blue red and green
BMy favorite colors are blue red, and green.
CMy favorite colors are blue, red, and green.
Dmy favorite Colors are Blue, Red, and Green.
✓ C) My favorite colors are blue, red, and green.
Explanation

Correct: capital M, commas between all list items, period at end.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Missing capital, commas, and period.
B)Missing comma after "blue".
D)Wrong capitalization (colors shouldn't be capitalized).
Q16

Which reason fits: "Drawing is fun. ___. For example, I drew my family yesterday."

APaper is made from wood.
BIt lets you express your creativity.
CSome people cannot draw.
DDrawing requires pencils.
✓ B) It lets you express your creativity.
Explanation

Drawing a family picture is an example of expressing creativity - the reason connects to the example.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Irrelevant to why drawing is fun.
C)Negative - doesn't support the topic.
D)Just a fact - doesn't explain why it's fun.

Reading Comprehension - Part 2

Reading: "Grandpa's Stories" (Q17-18)

I climb onto Grandpa's lap every evening. His voice is soft and warm like a cozy blanket. He tells me stories about dragons, princes, and faraway lands.

Sometimes I fall asleep before the story ends. When I wake up, I'm always in my own bed with my teddy bear beside me. Grandpa must have carried me there. Those are my favorite times.

Q17

Who is telling this story?

AGrandpa
BA dragon
CA grandchild
DThe teddy bear
✓ C) A grandchild
Explanation

"I climb onto Grandpa's lap" - the narrator sits on Grandpa's lap, so they are a grandchild.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Grandpa tells stories to the narrator.
B)Dragons are in the stories, not the narrator.
D)The teddy bear is just in the bed.
Q18

What makes these moments special to the narrator?

AThe teddy bear
BThe cozy time spent with Grandpa and his stories
CGoing to bed early
DThe dragons in the stories
✓ B) The cozy time spent with Grandpa and his stories
Explanation

"Those are my favorite times" - the warm, cozy moments with Grandpa make it special.

Why other options are wrong:
A)The teddy bear is nice, but not the main reason.
C)Bedtime isn't what makes it special.
D)The dragons are just part of stories, not the main point.

Entertainment & Conversation

Q19

Entertain with a time joke. What should you write?

AClocks have two hands.
BWhy did the boy throw his clock out the window? Because he wanted to see time fly!
CThere are 24 hours in a day.
DSome clocks are digital.
✓ B) Why did the boy throw his clock out the window? Because he wanted to see time fly!
Explanation

A pun on "time flies" - usually means time passes quickly, but here taken literally!

Why other options are wrong:
A)Just a fact - not funny.
C)Just information - not entertaining.
D)Just a fact - not a joke.
Q20

Friend: "I can't figure out this puzzle." You: ___

APuzzles are a waste of time.
BLet me help you! Two heads are better than one.
CI don't like puzzles either.
DJust give up then.
✓ B) Let me help you! Two heads are better than one.
Explanation

Offering help and using an encouraging saying - this is a friendly, helpful response!

Why other options are wrong:
A)Rude and unhelpful.
C)Doesn't help the friend.
D)Discouraging - bad friend response!
