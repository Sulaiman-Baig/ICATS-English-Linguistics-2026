Reading Passage: "The Thank You Project" (Q1-5)

Ms. Fatima gave her class a special assignment. "Write a thank you letter to someone who has helped you," she said.

Some students wrote to their parents. Others wrote to teachers or coaches. Zain decided to write to the school janitor, Mr. Riaz.

"You always keep our school clean and smile at us every morning," Zain wrote. "Thank you for making our school a happy place."

When Mr. Riaz received the letter, he had tears in his eyes. "In twenty years, this is the first thank you letter I have ever received," he said.

The class learned that kindness matters, especially to those who often go unnoticed.

Q1 What assignment did Ms. Fatima give?
Correct Answer: B) Write a thank you letter
"Write a thank you letter to someone who has helped you."
Q2 Who did Zain write his letter to?
Correct Answer: C) The school janitor
"Zain decided to write to the school janitor, Mr. Riaz."
Q3 Why did Zain thank Mr. Riaz?
Correct Answer: B) For keeping the school clean and being kind
"You always keep our school clean and smile at us every morning."
Q4 How did Mr. Riaz react to the letter?
Correct Answer: C) He had tears of happiness
"When Mr. Riaz received the letter, he had tears in his eyes."
Q5 What did the class learn from this project?
Correct Answer: B) Kindness matters, especially to those often overlooked
"Kindness matters, especially to those who often go unnoticed."

Poem: "My Schoolbag" (Q6-8)

My schoolbag is heavy, full of things,
Books and pencils, rulers and strings.
I carry it on my back each day,
As I walk to school and play.

Inside are my favorite books,
Notebooks filled with all my looks.
Erasers, crayons, scissors too,
Everything I need to get me through!

Q6 How does the speaker describe the schoolbag?
Correct Answer: C) Heavy
"My schoolbag is heavy, full of things."
Q7 What are some things inside the schoolbag?
Correct Answer: B) Books, pencils, rulers, strings, erasers, crayons, scissors
The poem lists all these items inside the schoolbag.
Q8 Complete the poem with a word that rhymes: "I zip it up and off I go, To school where I can learn and ___________."
Correct Answer: B) grow
"Grow" rhymes with "go."

Language Skills (Q9-16)

Q9 Which picture best matches the setting? (Fish in glass tanks, shark swimming overhead, guide)
Correct Answer: B) An aquarium
Fish in glass tanks, shark swimming overhead, guide = aquarium.
Q10 You want to persuade your teacher to have class outside. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) Having class outside would help us learn about nature firsthand. The fresh air might help us focus better too!
Persuasive writing with benefits and reasoning.
Q11 Put the sentences in order (space center visit story)
Correct Answer: C) 2, 3, 4, 1
Space center: arrived (2), learned about astronauts (3), saw rockets (4), watched blast off (1).
Q12 Add the best topic sentence (paragraph about bird nests)
Correct Answer: B) Birds build amazing nests.
Topic sentence about bird nests matches the paragraph.
Q13 What does "time flies" mean?
Correct Answer: B) Time seems to pass quickly
"Time flies" = time passes quickly.
Q14 What does this tell us about the book Zainab read?
Correct Answer: B) It was very engaging and exciting
"She couldn't put it down" - finished 200 pages in one day.
Q15 Choose the sentence with the correct punctuation.
Correct Answer: C) Did you see the bird in the tree?
Correct capitalization and question mark for a question.
Q16 Which reason fits best with the opinion and example? (Being honest)
Correct Answer: B) People trust and respect honest people.
Connects to the example about parents being proud.

Reading Passage: "Ocean Journey" (Q17-18)

The water is warm here near the equator. I have been swimming for months with my family. We are heading to colder waters where food is plentiful.

My baby swims close beside me. I sing to her in deep, low sounds that travel for miles through the water. Other families answer back. Together, we make the longest journey of any mammal on Earth.

Q17 Who is telling this story?
Correct Answer: C) A whale
Migrates, sings low sounds, mammal, longest journey = whale.
Q18 What clues tell you who the narrator is?
Correct Answer: A) The narrator migrates, sings in low sounds, is a mammal, and makes the longest journey
Classic whale characteristics.

Entertainment & Conversation (Q19-20)

Q19 You want to entertain someone with a joke about space. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) Why did the astronaut break up with his girlfriend? Because he needed more space!
A pun on "space" (room and outer space).
Q20 Choose the best reply: Parent says "Thank you for cleaning your room without being asked."
Correct Answer: B) You are welcome! I wanted to help out.
Polite and humble response to thanks.
