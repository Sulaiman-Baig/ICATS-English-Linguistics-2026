Reading Passage: "The Rainbow After Rain" (Q1-5)

Aisha was feeling sad because it was raining on her birthday. She had planned a garden party with her friends, but now they couldn't play outside.

"Don't worry," said her grandmother. "Rain brings special gifts."

After an hour, the rain stopped. Aisha looked out the window and gasped. A beautiful rainbow stretched across the sky! Its colors were so bright - red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple.

"Quick, everyone outside!" Aisha shouted. Her friends came running, and they all stood amazed under the rainbow. They played games until sunset, and Aisha said it was the best birthday ever.

"See?" smiled grandmother. "Sometimes the best surprises come after we wait patiently."

Q1

Why was Aisha sad at the beginning?

AShe didn't have any friends
BIt was raining on her birthday
CShe didn't get any presents
DHer grandmother was sick
✓ B) It was raining on her birthday
Explanation

The story says "Aisha was feeling sad because it was raining on her birthday. She had planned a garden party but couldn't play outside."

Why other options are wrong:
A)She had friends coming to her party.
C)Presents are not mentioned in the story.
D)Grandmother was there to comfort her, not sick.
Q2

What "special gift" did the rain bring?

AA cake
BNew toys
CA rainbow
DMore rain
✓ C) A rainbow
Explanation

"A beautiful rainbow stretched across the sky!" - this was the special gift that came after the rain stopped.

Why other options are wrong:
A)A cake is not mentioned in the story.
B)New toys are not mentioned.
D)The rain stopped, it didn't bring more rain.
Q3

How many colors of the rainbow are mentioned?

AFour
BFive
CSix
DSeven
✓ C) Six
Explanation

The story lists: "red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple" - that's six colors.

Why other options are wrong:
A)More than four colors are listed.
B)More than five colors are listed.
D)Only six colors are specifically mentioned.
Q4

How did Aisha feel at the end?

AStill sad
BVery happy
CAngry
DSleepy
✓ B) Very happy
Explanation

"Aisha said it was the best birthday ever" - this shows she was very happy at the end.

Why other options are wrong:
A)She said it was the best birthday - not sad.
C)No anger is mentioned in the story.
D)She was playing games, not sleepy.
Q5

What lesson does grandmother teach?

ARain is always bad
BNever have birthday parties
CGood things come to those who wait patiently
DRainbows are not real
✓ C) Good things come to those who wait patiently
Explanation

Grandmother said: "Sometimes the best surprises come after we wait patiently." This teaches patience brings rewards.

Why other options are wrong:
A)The story shows rain brought a gift - opposite message.
B)They had a great party - not the lesson.
D)The rainbow was real in the story.

Poem Section

Poem: "The Busy Bee" (Q6-8)

Buzz, buzz, buzz goes the little bee,
Flying from flower to flower with glee.
Collecting nectar all day long,
Humming a happy little song.

Back to the hive before it's dark,
Making honey, what a spark!
Sweet and golden, thick and yummy,
All thanks to the bee so funny.

Q6

What does the bee collect?

ALeaves
BNectar
CSeeds
DWater
✓ B) Nectar
Explanation

The poem says "Collecting nectar all day long" - nectar is what bees gather from flowers to make honey.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Leaves are not mentioned - bees don't collect leaves.
C)Seeds are not mentioned in the poem.
D)Water is not mentioned in the poem.
Q7

Where does the bee go at end of day?

ATo another garden
BTo sleep under a leaf
CBack to the hive
DTo the river
✓ C) Back to the hive
Explanation

The poem says "Back to the hive before it's dark" - bees return home to their hive.

Why other options are wrong:
A)It goes home, not to another garden.
B)Bees live in hives, not under leaves.
D)A river is not mentioned in the poem.
Q8

Complete the poem with a word that rhymes: "The flowers bloom in colors so bright, The garden is such a lovely ___________."

Aplace
Bsight
Cday
Dthing
✓ B) sight
Explanation

"Sight" rhymes with "bright" - both end with the "-ight" sound. A garden is indeed a lovely sight!

Why other options are wrong:
A)"Place" doesn't rhyme with "bright".
C)"Day" doesn't rhyme with "bright".
D)"Thing" doesn't rhyme with "bright".

Language Skills Section

Q9

Which picture best matches the setting?
"The snow was falling softly outside. Inside, the family gathered around the fireplace. Father was reading a story while everyone sipped warm soup."

AA summer beach scene
BA warm cozy room in winter
CA school classroom
DA busy marketplace
✓ B) A warm cozy room in winter
Explanation

Clues: snow falling, fireplace, warm soup = cozy winter setting inside a home.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Snow indicates winter, not summer beach.
C)Fireplaces are not in school classrooms.
D)The family is gathered inside, not in a marketplace.
Q10

You want to persuade your teacher to have class outside. What should you write?

AThe classroom has desks and chairs.
BLearning outside would be fun and we could study nature up close!
CTrees are green.
DSome schools don't have playgrounds.
✓ B) Learning outside would be fun and we could study nature up close!
Explanation

Persuasive writing gives reasons and benefits. This option explains WHY outside class is good.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Just states a fact - doesn't persuade.
C)Just a fact about trees - not persuasive.
D)Not related to convincing the teacher.
Q11

Put sentences in order: 1. After that, she put on her shoes. 2. First, Noor brushed her teeth. 3. Then, she ate her breakfast. 4. Finally, she went to school.

A2, 3, 1, 4
B1, 2, 3, 4
C4, 3, 2, 1
D2, 1, 3, 4
✓ A) 2, 3, 1, 4
Explanation

Follow sequence words: First (2), Then (3), After that (1), Finally (4).

Why other options are wrong:
B)"Finally" (4) comes at the end, not first.
C)Backwards order - "First" should be first.
D)"Then" (3) comes before "After that" (1).
Q12

Add the best topic sentence for sandwich instructions.

ASandwiches are healthy.
BHere is how to make a peanut butter and jelly sandwich.
CI like to eat lunch.
DBread is made from wheat.
✓ B) Here is how to make a peanut butter and jelly sandwich.
Explanation

A topic sentence introduces the main idea. This introduces the how-to instructions that follow.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Doesn't introduce instructions.
C)Too general - doesn't match the topic.
D)Irrelevant to making a sandwich.
Q13

What does "raining cats and dogs" mean?

ACats and dogs falling from sky
BIt was raining very heavily
CThere were pets outside
DAnimals playing in rain
✓ B) It was raining very heavily
Explanation

"Raining cats and dogs" is an idiom meaning very heavy rain - not literal animals!

Why other options are wrong:
A)It's an idiom - not literal meaning.
C)Not about real pets being outside.
D)Not about animals playing.
Q14

What does the narrator feel about visiting Grandma?

ABored and tired
BHappy and loving
CScared and nervous
DAngry and sad
✓ B) Happy and loving
Explanation

"I love visiting her more than anything" shows strong positive feelings.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Says "love visiting" - not bored.
C)No fear mentioned at all.
D)Opposite feelings - the narrator is happy.
Q15

Choose the sentence with correct punctuation.

Awhat a beautiful day it is!
BWhat a beautiful day it is.
CWhat a beautiful day it is!
Dwhat a Beautiful Day it is!
✓ C) What a beautiful day it is!
Explanation

Exclamations need: capital letter at start + exclamation mark at end.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Missing capital letter at start.
B)Exclamations need !, not a period.
D)Wrong capitalization throughout.
Q16

Which reason fits best: "Reading books is a great hobby. _______. For example, I learned about dinosaurs from a book."

ABooks have many pages.
BLibraries have thousands of books.
CBooks help us learn new things.
DSome books are very expensive.
✓ C) Books help us learn new things.
Explanation

The example mentions learning about dinosaurs - so the reason should be about learning.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Page count is irrelevant to the example.
B)Libraries are irrelevant to the example.
D)Cost is irrelevant to the example.

Reading Comprehension - Part 2

Reading: "A Strange Morning" (Q17-18)

I stretched my four legs and yawned. Time for my morning routine. First, I cleaned my face with my paw. Then, I walked to my food bowl and meowed loudly until my human filled it with yummy kibble. After breakfast, I found a sunny spot on the windowsill for my first nap of the day.

Q17

Who is telling this story?

AA dog
BA cat
CA child
DA bird
✓ B) A cat
Explanation

Clues: four legs, cleans with paw, meows, eats kibble, naps on windowsill = all cat behaviors!

Why other options are wrong:
A)Dogs bark, they don't meow.
C)Children don't have four legs or meow.
D)Birds don't have four legs.
Q18

What clues tell you who the narrator is?

AFour legs, cleans with paw, meows
BGoes to school
CFlies
DSwims
✓ A) Four legs, cleans with paw, meows
Explanation

These are all cat behaviors mentioned in the story that help identify the narrator.

Why other options are wrong:
B)School is not mentioned at all.
C)Flying is not mentioned - cats don't fly.
D)Swimming is not mentioned.

Entertainment & Conversation

Q19

You want to entertain someone with a joke about food. What should you write?

AWe need to eat food to stay healthy.
BWhat did the hungry computer eat? Microchips!
CVegetables are good for you.
DBreakfast is the first meal of the day.
✓ B) What did the hungry computer eat? Microchips!
Explanation

To entertain, you need humor! This is a pun - "microchips" sounds like a snack but is also a computer part.

Why other options are wrong:
A)This is information, not a joke.
C)This is information, not humor.
D)This is a fact, not entertaining.
Q20

Choose the best reply: Zahra: Would you like to come to my birthday party? Kamran: ___

AWhen is your birthday?
BI would love to! Thank you for inviting me.
CParties are loud.
DI already went to a party.
✓ B) I would love to! Thank you for inviting me.
Explanation

This is a polite, friendly acceptance of an invitation - shows good manners and enthusiasm.

Why other options are wrong:
A)She already said it's a party invitation.
C)Rude response - doesn't answer the invitation.
D)Irrelevant - doesn't respond to this invitation.
