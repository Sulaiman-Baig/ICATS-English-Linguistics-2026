Detailed Explanations

Reading Passage: "The Night Sky Show" (Q1-5)

"Wake up, wake up!" Dad whispered excitedly. It was the middle of the night, but Fatima jumped out of bed. Tonight was special - there was going to be a meteor shower!

They wrapped themselves in warm blankets and went to the backyard. The sky was clear and full of stars.

"Look!" Dad pointed. A streak of light shot across the sky, then another, and another. It was like the sky was raining stars!

"Make a wish!" said Dad.

Fatima closed her eyes and wished that she and Dad could have more nights like this together.

They stayed outside for an hour, counting shooting stars. Fatima counted twenty-three. It was the most magical night of her life.

Q1 Why did Dad wake Fatima up?
Correct Answer: B) There was going to be a meteor shower
"Tonight was special - there was going to be a meteor shower!"
Q2 Where did they go to watch the meteor shower?
Correct Answer: C) To the backyard
They "went to the backyard" to watch the meteor shower.
Q3 What did the meteors look like according to the story?
Correct Answer: B) Like streaks of light / raining stars
"A streak of light shot across the sky" and "It was like the sky was raining stars!"
Q4 What did Fatima wish for?
Correct Answer: C) More special nights with Dad
Fatima "wished that she and Dad could have more nights like this together."
Q5 How did Fatima feel about that night?
Correct Answer: C) It was the most magical night of her life
The story explicitly says "It was the most magical night of her life."

Poem: "The Snowman" (Q6-8)

We built a snowman, round and tall,
The biggest snowman of them all.
With coal for eyes and a carrot nose,
A hat on top and sticks for toes.

But when the sun came out to play,
Our snowman slowly melted away.
We waved goodbye to our snowy friend,
Until next winter, we'll meet again.

Q6 What was used for the snowman's nose?
Correct Answer: C) A carrot
"With coal for eyes and a carrot nose" - the nose was a carrot.
Q7 What happened when the sun came out?
Correct Answer: B) The snowman melted away
"When the sun came out to play, Our snowman slowly melted away."
Q8 Complete the poem with a word that rhymes: "We played in snow throughout the day, Building memories as we ___________."
Correct Answer: B) play
"Play" rhymes with "day" in "We played in snow throughout the day."

Language Skills (Q9-16)

Q9 Which picture best matches the setting? The referee blew the whistle. Players in two different colored jerseys ran onto the green field. The crowd in the stadium started cheering loudly.
Correct Answer: B) A football match
Referee, whistle, jerseys, green field, and stadium all indicate a football match.
Q10 You want to persuade your parents to get a puppy. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) A puppy would be a great companion and I promise to take care of it every day!
This is persuasive - it gives a benefit (companion) and shows responsibility (promise to take care).
Q11 Put the sentences in order: 1. Then, I add soap and water. 2. First, I put dirty clothes in the machine. 3. Next, I press the start button. 4. Finally, the clothes come out clean.
Correct Answer: A) 2, 1, 3, 4
Laundry: First (2) put clothes in, Then (1) add soap, Next (3) press start, Finally (4) clean clothes.
Q12 Read the paragraph and add the best topic sentence: ___. Its trunk can grow to be very thick. Baobab trees can store water in their trunks during dry seasons. Some of these trees are over 1,000 years old!
Correct Answer: B) The baobab tree is one of the most amazing trees in the world.
This topic sentence introduces the paragraph about the amazing baobab tree.
Q13 What does "hit the sack" mean in: After playing all day, Yusuf was ready to hit the sack.
Correct Answer: B) Yusuf was ready to go to sleep
"Hit the sack" is an idiom meaning to go to bed or go to sleep.
Q14 The family had planned a hiking trip for weeks. But when Saturday came, rain poured down all day. Instead of being sad, they made hot cocoa and played board games together. What lesson can we learn from this family?
Correct Answer: C) You can still have fun when plans change
The family made the best of a rainy day with cocoa and games instead of being sad.
Q15 Choose the sentence with the correct punctuation.
Correct Answer: B) We visited Karachi, Islamabad, and Lahore.
Correct capitalization (proper nouns), commas between items in a list, and proper ending.
Q16 Which reason fits best? Learning to ride a bike is exciting. ___. For example, once I learned, I could ride to my friend's house by myself.
Correct Answer: B) It gives you freedom and independence.
This connects "exciting" with the example of riding to a friend's house alone.

Reading Passage: "Under the Sea" (Q17-18)

I swished my tail and glided through the water. Today, I was searching for the shiniest shell to give to my friend on her birthday. The coral reef was full of beautiful things.

Suddenly, I saw it - a pearl-colored shell gleaming in the sand. Perfect! I grabbed it carefully and swam home as fast as my fins could take me.

Q17 Who is telling this story?
Correct Answer: B) A fish
The narrator has a tail, fins, swims, and lives underwater - it's a fish.
Q18 What clues tell you who the narrator is?
Correct Answer: A) The narrator has a tail and fins and swims
"Swished my tail," "my fins," and gliding through water are fish characteristics.

Entertainment & Conversation (Q19-20)

Q19 You want to entertain someone with a joke about sports. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) Why couldn't the bicycle stand up by itself? Because it was two-tired!
Wordplay joke - "two-tired" sounds like "too tired" and bikes have two tires.
Q20 Choose the best reply: Guest: Thank you for having me over. Host: ___
Correct Answer: B) You're welcome! Come again anytime.
A polite, welcoming response to a thank you from a guest.
