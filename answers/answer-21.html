Detailed Explanations

Reading Passage: "The Lost Teddy Bear" (Q1-5)

Ayesha loved her teddy bear, Mr. Buttons, more than anything. She had him since she was a baby. One day, after a trip to the mall, Mr. Buttons was missing!

Ayesha searched everywhere - the car, her room, the living room. Nothing. She cried herself to sleep that night.

The next morning, the phone rang. It was the mall security. "We found a teddy bear with a name tag that says Ayesha. Is it yours?"

Ayesha and her mom rushed to the mall. There was Mr. Buttons, sitting on a chair in the security office, looking like he was waiting for her!

Q1 What was the name of Ayesha's teddy bear?
Correct Answer: C) Mr. Buttons
"Ayesha loved her teddy bear, Mr. Buttons."
Q2 Where did Ayesha lose her teddy bear?
Correct Answer: B) At the mall
"After a trip to the mall, Mr. Buttons was missing!"
Q3 How did the security know the teddy bear was Ayesha's?
Correct Answer: B) It had a name tag with her name
"We found a teddy bear with a name tag that says Ayesha."
Q4 How did Ayesha feel when she lost Mr. Buttons?
Correct Answer: C) Very sad
"She cried herself to sleep that night."
Q5 What lesson can we learn from this story?
Correct Answer: B) Name tags on belongings can help return lost items
The name tag helped security return the teddy bear.

Poem: "Grandma's Cookies" (Q6-8)

The smell of cookies fills the air,
Grandma's baking with such care.
Chocolate chips in every bite,
Golden brown, they are just right!

She lets me help her stir the dough,
And watch the timer as minutes go.
The best part is when they are done,
Eating them together is so much fun!

Q6 What kind of cookies is Grandma making?
Correct Answer: B) Chocolate chip cookies
"Chocolate chips in every bite."
Q7 What does the child help Grandma do?
Correct Answer: C) Stir the dough
"She lets me help her stir the dough."
Q8 Complete the poem with a word that rhymes: "We sit together, Grandma and me, Having cookies with a cup of ___________."
Correct Answer: B) tea
"Tea" rhymes with "me."

Language Skills (Q9-16)

Q9 Which picture best matches the setting? "Colorful slides and swings filled the playground. Children were laughing and playing while parents watched from nearby benches."
Correct Answer: A) A park playground
Slides, swings, children playing, parents watching = playground.
Q10 You want to persuade your parents to get you a bicycle. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) A bicycle would help me exercise and I could ride to my friend's house safely!
Persuasive with benefits (exercise, visiting friends).
Q11 Put the sentences in order: 1. Finally, I put the letter in the mailbox. 2. First, I wrote a letter to my grandparents. 3. Next, I put the letter in an envelope. 4. Then, I put a stamp on the envelope.
Correct Answer: A) 2, 3, 4, 1
Letter: wrote (2), envelope (3), stamp (4), mailbox (1).
Q12 Add the best topic sentence: "_______. They help us see when it is dark. Flashlights are used for camping and during power outages. Always keep batteries ready for your flashlight."
Correct Answer: B) Flashlights are useful tools.
Topic sentence about flashlights matches the paragraph.
Q13 What does "let sleeping dogs lie" mean? "When his sister asked about the broken vase, Dad said to let sleeping dogs lie."
Correct Answer: B) Do not bring up an old problem that might cause trouble
"Let sleeping dogs lie" = avoid stirring up old issues.
Q14 What does this story tell us about the family? "The family spread out their picnic blanket at the park. Just as they opened the food basket, raindrops started falling! They quickly packed up and had their picnic in the car instead, laughing at their adventure."
Correct Answer: B) They can find fun in any situation
They had a car picnic and laughed about their adventure.
Q15 Choose the sentence with the correct punctuation.
Correct Answer: C) Can you pass me the salt, please?
Correct capitalization, comma before "please," question mark.
Q16 Which reason fits best? "Learning a new language is rewarding. _______. For example, I can now talk to my grandmother who only speaks Urdu."
Correct Answer: B) It helps you communicate with more people.
Connects to example of talking to grandmother.

Reading Passage: "Morning Swim" (Q17-18)

The sun is rising over the ice. I slide on my belly and dive into the freezing water. Brrr! But I love it. I catch a fish for breakfast and pop back up onto the ice.

My fluffy babies are waiting for me. I give them some fish and we huddle together to stay warm. Life at the South Pole is cold but wonderful!

Q17 Who is telling this story?
Correct Answer: B) A penguin
Slides on belly, catches fish, South Pole = penguin.
Q18 What clues tell you who the narrator is?
Correct Answer: A) The narrator slides on its belly, dives for fish, has fluffy babies, and lives at the South Pole
Classic penguin characteristics.

Entertainment & Conversation (Q19-20)

Q19 You want to entertain someone with a joke about fruit. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) Why did the banana go to the doctor? Because it was not peeling well!
A pun on "peeling" (banana skin) and "feeling."
Q20 Choose the best reply: Friend: "Can you help me with my homework?" You: ___
Correct Answer: B) Sure! What do you need help with?
Helpful and friendly response.
