Reading Passage: "The Wishing Well" (Q1-5)

In the middle of the village stood an old well. People said that if you threw a coin in and made a wish, it would come true.

Little Zoya had only one coin. She thought carefully about what to wish for. "I could wish for toys," she thought. "Or candy. Or a new dress."

Then she saw old Mr. Khan struggling to carry his groceries. His back was bent, and he looked very tired.

Zoya walked to the well, tossed her coin, and whispered, "I wish for Mr. Khan to feel better."

The next week, the village doctor offered free checkups. Mr. Khan's health improved, and he smiled more than ever before. Zoya never knew if it was magic or coincidence, but she felt happier than any toy could make her.

Q1 What was special about the well?
Correct Answer: B) People believed wishes made there came true
The story states "People said that if you threw a coin in and made a wish, it would come true."
Q2 What did Zoya wish for?
Correct Answer: D) Mr. Khan's health to improve
Zoya whispered, "I wish for Mr. Khan to feel better" after seeing him struggling.
Q3 Why did Zoya choose to make that wish?
Correct Answer: B) She saw Mr. Khan struggling and wanted to help
Zoya saw Mr. Khan struggling with groceries and his bent back, so she chose to help him instead of wishing for toys.
Q4 What happened after Zoya made her wish?
Correct Answer: C) Mr. Khan's health improved
The village doctor offered free checkups and Mr. Khan's health improved.
Q5 What does this story teach us?
Correct Answer: B) Kindness and thinking of others brings true happiness
Zoya "felt happier than any toy could make her" - showing that helping others brings greater happiness than material things.

Poem: "The Ocean" (Q6-8)

The ocean is wide, the ocean is deep,
Full of creatures and secrets to keep.
Dolphins that dance and whales that sing,
Fish of all colors, each a beautiful thing.

Waves that crash upon the shore,
Each one different from the one before.
The ocean calls me to come and play,
I could swim in its waters every day.

Q6 What creatures are mentioned in the poem?
Correct Answer: B) Dolphins, whales, and fish
The poem mentions "Dolphins that dance and whales that sing, Fish of all colors."
Q7 What do the whales do according to the poem?
Correct Answer: B) Sing
The poem says "whales that sing" - dolphins dance, while whales sing.
Q8 Complete the poem with a word that rhymes: "Seashells scattered along the sand, I pick one up with my little ___________."
Correct Answer: C) hand
"Hand" rhymes with "sand" in "Seashells scattered along the sand."

Language Skills (Q9-16)

Q9 Which picture best matches the setting? The tent was set up near the trees. Stars twinkled above as the family sat around the campfire, roasting marshmallows.
Correct Answer: B) A campsite at night
Tent, trees, stars, campfire, and marshmallows all indicate camping at night.
Q10 You want to persuade your parents to let you stay up late. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) If I finish my homework and chores early, may I please stay up a little longer tonight?
This is persuasive because it offers responsible behavior in exchange for the request.
Q11 Put the sentences in order: 1. Finally, the chick broke out of its shell. 2. First, the mother hen laid an egg. 3. Next, she sat on the egg to keep it warm. 4. Then, the baby inside started to grow.
Correct Answer: A) 2, 3, 4, 1
Egg cycle: First (2) hen lays egg, Next (3) sits on it, Then (4) baby grows, Finally (1) chick breaks out.
Q12 Read the paragraph and add the best topic sentence: ___. There are eight planets in our solar system. The closest planet to the sun is Mercury. The largest planet is Jupiter. Earth is the only planet known to have life.
Correct Answer: B) Our solar system is full of amazing planets.
This topic sentence introduces the paragraph about planets in our solar system.
Q13 What does "a piece of my mind" mean in: Mom gave the messy kids a piece of her mind about cleaning up.
Correct Answer: B) Mom told them firmly what she thought
"Give someone a piece of your mind" means to tell them clearly and firmly what you think, usually when upset.
Q14 The children were playing happily in the park when dark clouds covered the sky. Rain started pouring down, so everyone ran to the shelter laughing and splashing through puddles. What is this passage mainly about?
Correct Answer: B) A sudden rainstorm during playtime
The passage describes children playing when rain suddenly started - a sudden rainstorm during playtime.
Q15 Choose the sentence with the correct punctuation.
Correct Answer: B) Sara asked, "Can I have some water?"
Correct punctuation: capital letters, comma before quote, quotation marks, question mark inside quotes.
Q16 Which reason fits best? Walking to school is good for you. ___. For example, it gives you exercise and fresh air.
Correct Answer: B) It helps keep your body healthy.
This reason connects "walking is good for you" with the example about exercise and fresh air.

Reading Passage: "The Surprise Visitor" (Q17-18)

I heard a knock at the door and rushed to open it. There stood Grandma with a big suitcase and an even bigger smile!

"Grandma! What are you doing here?" I shouted with joy.

"Surprise! I'm staying for a whole month," she said, giving me the warmest hug.

That night, we baked cookies together, just like old times. I fell asleep with a full tummy and an even fuller heart.

Q17 Who is telling this story?
Correct Answer: B) A grandchild
The narrator calls the visitor "Grandma" and receives a hug - this is from a grandchild's perspective.
Q18 How does the narrator feel about Grandma's visit?
Correct Answer: C) Very happy and joyful
The narrator "shouted with joy" and ended with "an even fuller heart" - clearly very happy.

Entertainment & Conversation (Q19-20)

Q19 You want to entertain someone with a joke about vegetables. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) What's a vegetable's favorite martial art? Karate, because it's all about the "chop"!
This is a joke using wordplay - "chop" relates to both karate and chopping vegetables.
Q20 Choose the best reply: Friend: I'm feeling really sad today. You: ___
Correct Answer: C) I'm sorry to hear that. Do you want to talk about it?
This shows empathy and offers support - the appropriate response to a sad friend.
