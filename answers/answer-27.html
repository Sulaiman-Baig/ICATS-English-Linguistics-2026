Detailed Explanations

Reading Passage: "The Wrong Bus" (Q1-5)

It was Farhan's first day taking the bus alone. He was nervous but excited. When the bus came, he hopped on and found a seat.

After a while, Farhan noticed the streets looked unfamiliar. "Excuse me," he asked the driver, "does this bus go to Green Street?"

"Green Street? Oh no, son, this is the Blue Route. You need the Red Route."

Farhan felt his heart pound. But the kind driver helped him. She called the other bus and told them to wait. When they reached the next stop, the Red Route bus was waiting!

"Thank you so much!" Farhan said.

"No problem," smiled the driver. "Everyone makes mistakes. The important thing is asking for help."

Q1 Why was Farhan nervous at the beginning?
Correct Answer: B) It was his first day taking the bus alone
"It was Farhan's first day taking the bus alone."
Q2 What mistake did Farhan make?
Correct Answer: B) He got on the wrong bus
"This is the Blue Route. You need the Red Route."
Q3 How did Farhan realize he was on the wrong bus?
Correct Answer: B) The streets looked unfamiliar
"Farhan noticed the streets looked unfamiliar."
Q4 How did the bus driver help Farhan?
Correct Answer: B) She called the right bus and had it wait
"She called the other bus and told them to wait."
Q5 What lesson did Farhan learn?
Correct Answer: B) It is okay to make mistakes and ask for help
"Everyone makes mistakes. The important thing is asking for help."

Poem: "The Train" (Q6-8)

Choo, choo, here comes the train,
Through sunshine, snow, or rain.
It carries people far and wide,
Come along and take a ride!

Past the mountains, through the plains,
Nothing stops the mighty trains.
Click-clack on the tracks so long,
Singing its own travel song.

Q6 What sounds does the train make?
Correct Answer: B) Choo, choo and click-clack
"Choo, choo" and "Click-clack on the tracks."
Q7 According to the poem, where does the train go?
Correct Answer: C) Past mountains and through plains
"Past the mountains, through the plains."
Q8 Complete the poem with a word that rhymes: "The train whistle blows loud and clear, It is a sound I love to ___________."
Correct Answer: B) hear
"Hear" rhymes with "clear."

Language Skills (Q9-16)

Q9 Which picture best matches the setting? (Baker, fresh bread, oven, pastries)
Correct Answer: B) A bakery
Baker, fresh bread, oven, pastries, display case = bakery.
Q10 You want to persuade your friend to join a book club with you. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) Join the book club with me! We will discover amazing stories and have fun discussions!
Persuasive with enthusiasm and benefits.
Q11 Put the sentences in order (vet visit story)
Correct Answer: A) 2, 4, 1, 3
Vet visit: took cat (2), waited (4), check-up (1), went home (3).
Q12 Add the best topic sentence (paragraph about microscopes)
Correct Answer: B) Microscopes are important tools for science.
Topic sentence about microscopes matches the paragraph.
Q13 What does "raining on someone's parade" mean?
Correct Answer: B) Do not spoil my happiness
"Raining on someone's parade" = ruining their happiness.
Q14 How did the family feel about the long wait at the restaurant?
Correct Answer: B) It was worth it for the good food
"The delicious food made the wait worthwhile."
Q15 Choose the sentence with the correct punctuation.
Correct Answer: C) Wow! That is the biggest cake I have ever seen!
Correct exclamation marks and capitalization.
Q16 Which reason fits best with the opinion and example? (Walking is good for you)
Correct Answer: B) It keeps your body active and improves your mood.
Connects to example about feeling energetic after walking.

Reading Passage: "Life on Eight Legs" (Q17-18)

I am not a bug, even though people think so! I have eight legs, not six like insects. I spend my days spinning beautiful webs and waiting patiently.

Some people are scared of me, but I am actually helpful. I eat mosquitoes and flies that bother humans. Think of me as a tiny helper, not a scary creature!

Q17 Who is telling this story?
Correct Answer: C) A spider
Eight legs, spins webs, eats flies = spider.
Q18 What clues tell you who the narrator is?
Correct Answer: A) The narrator has eight legs, spins webs, and eats flies
Classic spider characteristics.

Entertainment & Conversation (Q19-20)

Q19 You want to entertain someone with a joke about the moon. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) How does the man in the moon cut his hair? Eclipse it!
A pun on "eclipse" and "he clips it."
Q20 Choose the best reply: Friend says "I am worried about the test tomorrow."
Correct Answer: B) I am sure you will do great! Let us study together tonight.
Supportive and helpful response to a worried friend.
