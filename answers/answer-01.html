Reading Passage: "The Lost Puppy" (Q1-5)

Maya was walking home from school when she heard a soft whimper. She looked around and found a small brown puppy hiding under a bush. The puppy was shivering and looked scared.

"Don't worry, little one," Maya said softly. She gently picked up the puppy and noticed it had a blue collar with a tag. The tag said "Buddy" and had a phone number.

Maya took the puppy home and asked her mother to call the number. Soon, a happy little boy named Ali came to pick up his puppy. "Thank you so much!" Ali said with tears of joy. "Buddy ran away this morning and I was so worried!"

Maya felt warm inside. Helping others felt wonderful.

Q1

Where did Maya find the puppy?

AIn her backyard
BUnder a bush
CAt the park
DIn her school
✓ B) Under a bush
Explanation

The story clearly states that Maya "found a small brown puppy hiding under a bush" while walking home from school. This is directly mentioned in the first paragraph.

Why other options are wrong:
A)The story says she was walking home from school, not in her backyard.
C)The park is not mentioned anywhere in the story.
D)She found the puppy on her way home, not in school.
Q2

How did Maya know the puppy's name?

AThe puppy told her
BShe guessed it
CIt was written on the collar tag
DAli told her
✓ C) It was written on the collar tag
Explanation

Maya noticed that the puppy "had a blue collar with a tag. The tag said 'Buddy'" - this is how she learned the puppy's name before meeting Ali.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Puppies cannot talk - this is not realistic.
B)The story specifically mentions the tag, she didn't guess.
D)She knew the name before meeting Ali from the tag.
Q3

Why was the puppy shivering?

AIt was hot outside
BIt was playing a game
CIt was scared
DIt was happy
✓ C) It was scared
Explanation

The text directly tells us "The puppy was shivering and looked scared." Being lost and alone would make a puppy feel frightened. Shivering is a sign of fear or being cold.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Shivering indicates cold or fear, not heat.
B)No game was mentioned - the puppy was hiding.
D)The story says it looked scared, not happy.
Q4

How did Ali feel when he got his puppy back?

AAngry
BSad
CJoyful
DScared
✓ C) Joyful
Explanation

Ali said "Thank you so much!" with "tears of joy" - these are clear signs of happiness and relief at getting his beloved puppy back safely.

Why other options are wrong:
A)He was grateful, not angry - he thanked Maya.
B)He had "tears of joy," not sadness.
D)He was relieved and happy, not scared.
Q5

What lesson can we learn from this story?

ADon't talk to strangers
BHelping others makes us feel good
CPuppies are dangerous
DAlways run away from animals
✓ B) Helping others makes us feel good
Explanation

The story ends with "Maya felt warm inside. Helping others felt wonderful." This is the main moral lesson - when we help others, it makes us feel happy too.

Why other options are wrong:
A)This is not the theme - Maya helped a stranger.
C)The story shows puppies as friendly, not dangerous.
D)Maya helped the puppy, she didn't run away.

Poem Section

Poem: "My Little Garden" (Q6-8)

I planted a seed in the ground so deep,
I gave it water and let it sleep.
The sun came out with a golden glow,
And soon my little plant began to grow.

First came leaves of emerald green,
The prettiest sight I've ever seen.
Then a flower bloomed so bright,
Red and yellow, what a delight!

Q6

What did the speaker plant?

AA flower
BA tree
CA seed
DA leaf
✓ C) A seed
Explanation

The first line of the poem says "I planted a seed in the ground so deep." The seed later grew into a plant with flowers.

Why other options are wrong:
A)The flower grew from the seed - it wasn't planted.
B)A tree is not mentioned in the poem.
D)Leaves grew from the seed - they weren't planted.
Q7

What helped the plant grow?

AWater and sunlight
BOnly water
COnly sunlight
DNothing
✓ A) Water and sunlight
Explanation

The poem mentions both "I gave it water" and "The sun came out with a golden glow" - both water and sunlight helped the plant grow.

Why other options are wrong:
B)Sun is also mentioned - not just water.
C)Water is also mentioned - not just sunlight.
D)Both water and sun are mentioned in the poem.
Q8

Complete the poem with a word that rhymes: "The butterfly came to say hello, Dancing in the breeze so ___________."

Afast
Bslow
Chigh
Dbig
✓ B) slow
Explanation

"Hello" and "slow" rhyme because they both end with the "oh" sound. A butterfly dancing "slowly" in the breeze also makes sense - butterflies move gently.

Why other options are wrong:
A)"Fast" doesn't rhyme with "hello".
C)"High" doesn't rhyme with "hello".
D)"Big" doesn't rhyme with "hello".

Language Skills Section

Q9

Which picture best matches the setting?
"Sara sat by the window watching the raindrops race down the glass. Thunder rumbled outside while she sipped her hot cocoa and read her favorite book."

AA sunny beach
BA cozy room on a rainy day
CA playground
DA classroom
✓ B) A cozy room on a rainy day
Explanation

The clues are: "window," "raindrops," "thunder," "hot cocoa," and "read her favorite book." All these point to being inside a cozy room while it's raining outside.

Why other options are wrong:
A)It's raining with thunder - not sunny.
C)Sara is inside with a book, not at a playground.
D)She has hot cocoa - this sounds like home, not school.
Q10

You want to persuade your friend to read a book. What should you write?

AI finished reading the book yesterday.
BThe book has 100 pages.
CThis book is so exciting that you won't be able to put it down!
DBooks are made of paper.
✓ C) This book is so exciting that you won't be able to put it down!
Explanation

Persuasive writing uses exciting, emotional language to convince someone. Option C uses words like "so exciting" and "won't be able to put it down" to make the book sound appealing.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Just states a fact - doesn't persuade.
B)Just information - not convincing.
D)Irrelevant fact about paper - not persuasive.
Q11

Put the following sentences in order from beginning to end.
1. Finally, I blew out the candles and made a wish.
2. First, my mom baked a chocolate cake.
3. Then, my friends came to my party.
4. After that, we played games and ate pizza.

A2, 3, 4, 1
B1, 2, 3, 4
C3, 2, 1, 4
D2, 4, 3, 1
✓ A) 2, 3, 4, 1
Explanation

Look for the sequence words: "First" (mom baked - 2), "Then" (friends came - 3), "After that" (played games - 4), "Finally" (blew candles - 1). The order is 2, 3, 4, 1.

Why other options are wrong:
B)"Finally" (1) comes at the end, not first.
C)"First" (2) should come at the beginning.
D)"Then" (3) comes before "After that" (4).
Q12

Read the following paragraph. Then, add the best topic sentence.
"_______________________. They have long necks that help them eat leaves from tall trees. They also have spotted coats that help them hide from predators. Baby giraffes can stand up within an hour of being born."

AGiraffes are interesting animals with special features.
BZebras have stripes on their bodies.
CElephants are the largest land animals.
DAll animals live in the jungle.
✓ A) Giraffes are interesting animals with special features.
Explanation

A topic sentence introduces the main idea. The paragraph talks about giraffe features (long necks, spotted coats, baby giraffes), so the topic sentence should be about giraffes.

Why other options are wrong:
B)The paragraph is about giraffes, not zebras.
C)The paragraph is about giraffes, not elephants.
D)Too general - doesn't match the specific topic.
Q13

What does "butterflies in my stomach" mean in this sentence?
"Before the singing competition, Zara had butterflies in her stomach."

AZara ate butterflies
BZara felt nervous and excited
CZara saw butterflies outside
DZara had a stomach ache from food
✓ B) Zara felt nervous and excited
Explanation

"Butterflies in my stomach" is an idiom (expression) meaning to feel nervous or excited. Before a competition, it's normal to feel this fluttering, nervous feeling.

Why other options are wrong:
A)It's an idiom - not literal butterflies!
C)It refers to a feeling, not actual butterflies.
D)The feeling is from nervousness, not food.
Q14

Read the passage and answer the question.
"Ahmed loves to help at home. Every Saturday, he cleans his room, does his homework, and then watches TV as a reward. His parents are very proud of him."

What kind of person is Ahmed?

ALazy and careless
BResponsible and hardworking
CAngry and upset
DScared and worried
✓ B) Responsible and hardworking
Explanation

Ahmed "loves to help," "cleans his room," and "does his homework" regularly. His parents are "very proud of him." These are signs of being responsible and hardworking.

Why other options are wrong:
A)He does chores and homework - not lazy!
C)No evidence of anger - his parents are proud.
D)No evidence of fear or worry in the passage.
Q15

Choose the sentence with the correct punctuation.

Awhere is my pencil box
BWhere is my pencil box.
CWhere is my pencil box?
Dwhere Is My Pencil Box?
✓ C) Where is my pencil box?
Explanation

A question needs: 1) Capital letter at the start ("Where"), 2) Question mark at the end (?). Only option C has both correctly.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Missing capital letter and question mark.
B)Questions need ?, not a period.
D)Random capitals - only first word should be capitalized.
Q16

Which reason fits best with the opinion and example?
"Pizza is my favorite food. _______________________. For example, I love the cheese and tomato sauce on top."

APizza is round in shape.
BPizza is made in Italy.
CPizza tastes delicious to me.
DPizza is sold in many stores.
✓ C) Pizza tastes delicious to me.
Explanation

The opinion is "Pizza is my favorite food" and the example mentions loving "cheese and tomato sauce." The reason "tastes delicious" connects these logically - taste is why it's a favorite.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Shape isn't why it's a favorite food.
B)Origin doesn't explain personal preference.
D)Availability isn't why it's a favorite.

Reading Comprehension - Part 2

Reading: "At the Library" (Q17-18)

I love going to the library every Saturday. The shelves are full of colorful books waiting to be read. I always find a cozy corner and lose myself in amazing stories.

Today, I found a book about dinosaurs. The pictures were huge and the facts were incredible. Did you know that some dinosaurs were as tall as a five-story building?

Q17

Who is telling this story?

AA librarian
BA dinosaur
CThe reader/narrator who visits the library
DA book
✓ C) The reader/narrator who visits the library
Explanation

The story uses "I" - "I love going to the library." This first-person narrator is someone who visits the library to read books, not a librarian or anything else.

Why other options are wrong:
A)The narrator visits the library - they don't work there.
B)Dinosaurs can't tell stories or read books.
D)Books can't narrate - they are being read.
Q18

What is the story mostly about?

AHow to become a librarian
BSomeone's love for the library and reading
CHow dinosaurs lived
DHow to build a library
✓ B) Someone's love for the library and reading
Explanation

The main idea is the narrator's love of visiting the library: "I love going to the library," finding "a cozy corner," and getting "lost in amazing stories."

Why other options are wrong:
A)The story doesn't teach how to be a librarian.
C)Dinosaurs are just one book mentioned - not the main topic.
D)Building libraries is not mentioned at all.

Entertainment & Conversation

Q19

You want to entertain someone with a joke about elephants. What should you write?

AElephants are the largest land animals and can weigh up to 6 tons.
BWhat do you call an elephant that doesn't matter? An irrelephant!
CElephants live in Africa and Asia.
DBaby elephants are called calves.
✓ B) What do you call an elephant that doesn't matter? An irrelephant!
Explanation

To entertain, you need humor! Option B is a joke using a pun: "irrelephant" sounds like "irrelevant" (meaning doesn't matter), but includes "elephant." Puns are funny wordplay!

Why other options are wrong:
A)This is a fact, not a joke - not entertaining.
C)This is information, not humor.
D)This is a fact about baby elephants, not a joke.
Q20

Choose the best reply to complete the conversation.
Amira: I'm sorry I broke your crayon.
Bilal: ___________________

AThat's very bad of you!
BIt's okay. Accidents happen.
CI don't want to talk to you.
DCrayons are expensive.
✓ B) It's okay. Accidents happen.
Explanation

When someone apologizes for an accident, a kind response shows forgiveness. "It's okay. Accidents happen" is understanding, forgiving, and maintains the friendship.

Why other options are wrong:
A)Too harsh - Amira already apologized.
C)Unkind and doesn't accept the apology.
D)Makes Amira feel worse - not kind.
