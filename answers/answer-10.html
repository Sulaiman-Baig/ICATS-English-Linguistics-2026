Detailed Explanations

Reading Passage: "The Giving Tree" (Q1-5)

In the village, there stood a very old mango tree. It gave sweet mangoes to everyone who passed by. The children climbed its branches, and elders rested in its shade.

One hot summer, no rain came. The river dried up, and people were worried. The mango tree saw their struggle.

"I cannot make rain," thought the tree, "but I can still help."

The tree dropped extra leaves to cover the ground and keep it cool. It gave more fruit than ever before. People collected the mangoes and shared them with neighboring villages.

When the rains finally came, everyone gathered around the tree to say thank you. They decorated it with colorful ribbons and flowers.

"As long as I can give, I am happy," rustled the tree.

Q1 What did the mango tree give to people?
Correct Answer: C) Mangoes and shade
The tree "gave sweet mangoes" and "elders rested in its shade."
Q2 What problem did the village face?
Correct Answer: B) No rain and the river dried up
"One hot summer, no rain came. The river dried up."
Q3 How did the tree try to help during the drought?
Correct Answer: B) It dropped extra leaves and gave more fruit
The tree "dropped extra leaves to cover the ground and keep it cool. It gave more fruit than ever before."
Q4 How did the villagers show gratitude?
Correct Answer: C) They decorated it with ribbons and flowers
"They decorated it with colorful ribbons and flowers."
Q5 What is the main message of this story?
Correct Answer: B) Helping others brings happiness
"As long as I can give, I am happy" - the tree finds happiness in giving.

Poem: "My Bicycle" (Q6-8)

My bicycle takes me everywhere,
Through the streets without a care.
Pedaling fast, feeling free,
Oh, what fun it is for me!

Past the shops and through the park,
I ride until it's almost dark.
Wind in my hair and sun on my face,
My bicycle is my favorite place.

Q6 How does the speaker feel when riding the bicycle?
Correct Answer: B) Free and happy
"Feeling free" and "Oh, what fun it is for me!"
Q7 Where does the speaker ride the bicycle?
Correct Answer: B) Past shops and through the park
"Past the shops and through the park"
Q8 Complete the poem with a word that rhymes: "I ring my bell as I go by, Waving at friends beneath the ___________."
Correct Answer: B) sky
"Sky" rhymes with "by" in "I ring my bell as I go by."

Language Skills (Q9-16)

Q9 Which picture best matches the setting? The dentist asked me to open my mouth wide. The bright light above my head was shining. I could see tools on a small tray next to the chair.
Correct Answer: B) A dentist's office
Dentist, mouth wide open, bright light, tools on a tray - all describe a dentist's office.
Q10 You want to persuade your teacher to have less homework. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) With less homework, we would have more time to rest and play, which helps us focus better in class!
This is persuasive as it gives benefits of the suggestion.
Q11 Put the sentences in order: 1. Then, I spread peanut butter on one slice. 2. First, I took out two slices of bread. 3. Next, I put jelly on the other slice. 4. Finally, I put the two slices together.
Correct Answer: A) 2, 1, 3, 4
PB&J order: First (2) bread, Then (1) peanut butter, Next (3) jelly, Finally (4) together.
Q12 Read the paragraph and add the best topic sentence: ___. Bees collect nectar from flowers. They bring it back to the hive and turn it into honey. Honey is stored in honeycombs made of beeswax.
Correct Answer: B) Here is how bees make honey.
The paragraph explains the process of honey-making.
Q13 What does "zip your lip" mean in: Mom told us to zip our lips during the movie.
Correct Answer: B) Be quiet and stop talking
"Zip your lip" means to be quiet, like closing a zipper.
Q14 Sarah looked longingly out the window at her friends playing. But first, she had to clean her room and do her homework. She worked quickly and carefully, and soon she was outside playing too! What does this passage teach us?
Correct Answer: C) Finishing responsibilities first leads to rewards
Sarah finished her chores and homework, then got to play.
Q15 Choose the sentence with the correct punctuation.
Correct Answer: C) Wow! That was amazing!
Correct exclamation marks and capitalization.
Q16 Which reason fits best? Cooking is a useful skill to learn. ___. For example, I made scrambled eggs for breakfast yesterday.
Correct Answer: B) It helps you make your own meals and be independent.
This connects the opinion with the example of making breakfast.

Reading Passage: "The Dance Performance" (Q17-18)

The music started, and I leaped onto the stage. My heart was beating fast, but my feet knew exactly what to do. I twirled and jumped, moving with the melody.

The spotlight was warm on my face. I could see my parents in the front row, smiling proudly. When the music ended, the audience clapped loudly.

I bowed and felt tears of happiness in my eyes. All those hours of practice were worth it.

Q17 Who is telling this story?
Correct Answer: B) A dancer performing on stage
The narrator leaped onto stage, twirled and jumped, and bowed at the end.
Q18 How does the narrator feel at the end?
Correct Answer: C) Happy and proud
"Tears of happiness" and feeling the hours of practice were "worth it."

Entertainment & Conversation (Q19-20)

Q19 You want to entertain someone with a joke about school supplies. What should you write?
Correct Answer: B) Why did the pencil go to school? Because it wanted to be sharp!
Wordplay - "sharp" means both intelligent and having a sharp point (pencils need sharpening).
Q20 Choose the best reply: Teacher: You did very well on your test! Student: ___
Correct Answer: B) Thank you! I studied really hard.
A polite, humble response acknowledging the compliment and effort.
