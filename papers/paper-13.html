Read the following story and answer questions 1 through 5.

The Lost Library Book

Zara had borrowed a book from the school library three weeks ago. Now it was due, and she could not find it anywhere! She looked under her bed, in her closet, and behind the sofa. No book.

"I will have to tell the librarian," Zara said sadly. She imagined having to pay for the lost book with all her saved pocket money.

At school, she walked slowly to the library. "Mrs. Ahmed, I am so sorry. I lost my book," she confessed.

Mrs. Ahmed smiled kindly. "Which book was it, dear?"

"The Magic Tree," Zara answered.

Mrs. Ahmed laughed gently. "Zara, you returned that book last week! You were the first one to return it on Monday. Do you not remember?"

Zara's face turned bright red, but then she laughed too. What a relief!

Q1. What was Zara worried about?

Q2. Where did Zara look for the book?

Q3. What did Zara expect to happen when she told the librarian?

Q4. What was the truth about the book?

Q5. How did Zara feel at the end of the story?

Read the following poem and answer questions 6 through 8.

The Moon

The moon is like a silver ball,
Watching over one and all.
It glows so bright up in the night,
A lovely, peaceful, calming sight.

Sometimes it is round, sometimes it is thin,
A crescent with a sleepy grin.
But whatever shape the moon may take,
It is there for us when we are awake.

Q6. What is the moon compared to in the poem?

Q7. According to the poem, what shapes can the moon be?
