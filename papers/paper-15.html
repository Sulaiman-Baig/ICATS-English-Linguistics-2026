Reading Comprehension

Read the following story and answer questions 1 through 5.

The Class Pet

Ms. Fatima's class had a new pet - a hamster named Peanut. The children took turns feeding him and cleaning his cage. Peanut loved running on his wheel at night.

One morning, the cage door was open, and Peanut was gone! The class looked everywhere - in the book corner, under the desks, behind the cabinets. No Peanut.

Then, little Amina heard a soft sound coming from her backpack. She opened it carefully, and there was Peanut, curled up in her lunchbox!

"He probably smelled your sandwich," laughed Ms. Fatima.

From that day on, everyone made sure to lock the cage door tightly. And Amina always checked her bag before leaving school!

Q1 What kind of pet did the class have?
Q2 What did Peanut love to do at night?
Q3 Where did the class look for Peanut?
Q4 Where was Peanut found?
Q5 What did the class learn from this experience?

Poetry

Read the following poem and answer questions 6 through 8.

Bedtime

The stars come out, the moon appears,
Time for sleep, my little dears.
Brush your teeth and say goodnight,
Everything will be alright.

Snuggle deep beneath the sheets,
Dream of all your favorite treats.
Tomorrow brings a brand new day,
With lots of time to run and play.

Q6 What time of day is this poem about?
Q7 What should you do before sleeping according to the poem?
Q8 Complete the poem with a word that rhymes:
"Close your eyes and count some sheep,
Soon you will be fast ___________."
