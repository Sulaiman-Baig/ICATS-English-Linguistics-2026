Read the following story and answer questions 1 through 5.

The Magic Paintbrush

Omar found an old paintbrush in his grandfather's attic. When he painted a picture of an apple, something amazing happened - the apple became real!

"This must be a magic paintbrush!" Omar exclaimed.

At first, Omar painted toys and candy. But then he saw a news report about children who had no food.

Omar thought carefully. He painted plates full of healthy food and delivered them to the shelter.

"Thank you!" the children said, their eyes shining with happiness.

Omar realized that the magic paintbrush was best used to help others, not just himself.

Q1. Where did Omar find the paintbrush?

Q2. What was special about the paintbrush?

Q3. What did Omar paint first?

Q4. What made Omar decide to help others?

Q5. What did Omar learn about the magic paintbrush?

Read the following poem and answer questions 6 through 8.

My Best Friend

My best friend is always there,
Showing me that she does care.
We play together, laugh, and run,
Being friends is so much fun!

When I am sad, she cheers me up,
Like hot cocoa in a cup.
I am so lucky, can not you see,
To have a friend who's there for me!

Q6. What does the friend do when the speaker is sad?

Q7. What is the friend compared to?

Q8. Complete the poem with a word that rhymes:
"Friends like you are hard to find,
You are so sweet and very ___________."
