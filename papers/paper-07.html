Read the following story and answer questions 1 through 5.

The Wishing Well

In the middle of the village stood an old well. People said that if you threw a coin in and made a wish, it would come true.

Little Zoya had only one coin. She thought carefully about what to wish for. "I could wish for toys," she thought. "Or candy. Or a new dress."

Then she saw old Mr. Khan struggling to carry his groceries. His back was bent, and he looked very tired.

Zoya walked to the well, tossed her coin, and whispered, "I wish for Mr. Khan to feel better."

The next week, the village doctor offered free checkups. Mr. Khan's health improved, and he smiled more than ever before. Zoya never knew if it was magic or coincidence, but she felt happier than any toy could make her.

Q1. What was special about the well?

Q2. What did Zoya wish for?

Q3. Why did Zoya choose to make that wish?

Q4. What happened after Zoya made her wish?

Q5. What does this story teach us?

Read the following poem and answer questions 6 through 8.

The Ocean

The ocean is wide, the ocean is deep,
Full of creatures and secrets to keep.
Dolphins that dance and whales that sing,
Fish of all colors, each a beautiful thing.

Waves that crash upon the shore,
Each one different from the one before.
The ocean calls me to come and play,
I could swim in its waters every day.

Q6. What creatures are mentioned in the poem?

Q7. What do the whales do according to the poem?

The Surprise Visitor

I heard a knock at the door and rushed to open it. There stood Grandma with a big suitcase and an even bigger smile!

"Grandma! What are you doing here?" I shouted with joy.

"Surprise! I'm staying for a whole month," she said, giving me the warmest hug.

That night, we baked cookies together, just like old times. I fell asleep with a full tummy and an even fuller heart.

Q17. Who is telling this story?

Q18. How does the narrator feel about Grandma's visit?
