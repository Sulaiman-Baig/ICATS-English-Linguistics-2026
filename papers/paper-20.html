Read the following story and answer questions 1 through 5.

The Giving Boy

Every week, Hamza saved some of his allowance money in a jar. His friends spent their money on toys and candy, but Hamza had a different plan.

At the end of the year, Hamza had saved enough to buy warm blankets. He and his parents took them to a shelter where people without homes could stay warm in winter.

A little boy at the shelter hugged his new blanket tightly. "Thank you," he whispered. Hamza felt his heart grow bigger.

"Why do you give your money away?" his friend asked later.

"Because making others happy makes me happier than any toy ever could," Hamza replied.

Q1. What did Hamza do with his allowance money?

Q2. What did Hamza buy with his saved money?

Q3. Where did Hamza take the blankets?

Q4. How did the little boy at the shelter react?

Q5. What did Hamza learn about happiness?

Read the following poem and answer questions 6 through 8.

The Wind

The wind is a traveler that never rests,
Visiting mountains, valleys, and nests.
Sometimes it whispers, soft and light,
Sometimes it howls throughout the night.

It carries seeds to places far,
And pushes clouds past moon and star.
Though we cannot see its face,
The wind is felt in every place.

Q6. According to the poem, what does the wind do?

Q7. What does the wind carry according to the poem?

Q8. Complete the poem with a word that rhymes:
"The wind can be gentle, the wind can be strong,
It whistles a tune like a beautiful ___________."
