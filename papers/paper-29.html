Read the following story and answer questions 1 through 5.

The Art Contest

The school announced an art contest. The winner would have their artwork displayed in the town hall. Nadia spent hours on her painting of a sunset over the mountains.

On the day of the contest, Nadia saw the other entries. They looked so good! "I have no chance," she thought sadly.

When the results came, Nadia could not believe her ears. "First place goes to Nadia for her beautiful sunset painting!"

Her art teacher smiled. "See? I told you your work was special. Never compare yourself to others. Your unique vision is what makes art beautiful."

Q1. What was the prize for the art contest?

Q2. What did Nadia paint?

Q3. How did Nadia feel when she saw the other entries?

Q4. What place did Nadia win?

Q5. What did Nadia's teacher teach her?

Read the following poem and answer questions 6 through 8.

The Playground

Swings go high, slides go down,
The best place to play in town!
Running, jumping, having fun,
Playing until the day is done.

Monkey bars and seesaws too,
So many things that we can do.
Friends are laughing everywhere,
Happy voices fill the air.

Q6. What does the speaker think is the best place in town?

Q7. What fills the air at the playground?

Q8. Complete the poem with a word that rhymes:
