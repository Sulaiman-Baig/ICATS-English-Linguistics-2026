# PAPER 1 - ANSWER KEY

## Reading Passage: "The Lost Puppy"

**Q1. Where did Maya find the puppy?**
- A) In her backyard - Wrong: The story says she was walking home from school
- **B) Under a bush** - CORRECT: "found a small brown puppy hiding under a bush"
- C) At the park - Wrong: Not mentioned in the story
- D) In her school - Wrong: She found it on her way home

**Q2. How did Maya know the puppy's name?**
- A) The puppy told her - Wrong: Puppies cannot talk
- B) She guessed it - Wrong: The story specifically mentions a tag
- **C) It was written on the collar tag** - CORRECT: "The tag said 'Buddy'"
- D) Ali told her - Wrong: She knew the name before meeting Ali

**Q3. Why was the puppy shivering?**
- A) It was hot outside - Wrong: Shivering indicates cold or fear, not heat
- B) It was playing a game - Wrong: No game was mentioned
- **C) It was scared** - CORRECT: "The puppy was shivering and looked scared"
- D) It was happy - Wrong: The puppy was lost and frightened

**Q4. How did Ali feel when he got his puppy back?**
- A) Angry - Wrong: He was grateful, not angry
- B) Sad - Wrong: He had "tears of joy," not sadness
- **C) Joyful** - CORRECT: "tears of joy" and "Thank you so much!"
- D) Scared - Wrong: He was relieved and happy

**Q5. What lesson can we learn from this story?**
- A) Do not talk to strangers - Wrong: Not the main theme
- **B) Helping others makes us feel good** - CORRECT: "Maya felt warm inside. Helping others felt wonderful."
- C) Puppies are dangerous - Wrong: The story shows the opposite
- D) Always run away from animals - Wrong: Maya helped the puppy, not ran from it

## Poem: "My Little Garden"

**Q6. What did the speaker plant?**
- A) A flower - Wrong: Flowers grew from what was planted
- B) A tree - Wrong: Not mentioned
- **C) A seed** - CORRECT: "I planted a seed in the ground so deep"
- D) A leaf - Wrong: Not mentioned

**Q7. What helped the plant grow?**
- **A) Water and sunlight** - CORRECT: "I gave it water" and "The sun came out"
- B) Only water - Wrong: Sun is also mentioned
- C) Only sunlight - Wrong: Water is also mentioned
- D) Nothing - Wrong: Both water and sun are mentioned

**Q8. Complete the poem with a word that rhymes: "...beautiful ___________."**
- A) fast - Wrong: Does not rhyme with "hello"
- **B) slow** - CORRECT: Rhymes with "hello" and fits the context of a gentle breeze
- C) high - Wrong: Does not rhyme
- D) big - Wrong: Does not rhyme

**Q9. Which picture best matches the setting?**
- A) A sunny beach - Wrong: The story mentions rain and being inside
- **B) A cozy room on a rainy day** - CORRECT: "raindrops," "inside," "window," "hot cocoa"
- C) A playground - Wrong: Sara is inside
- D) A classroom - Wrong: Sara is at home with her book and cocoa

**Q10. You want to persuade your friend to read a book. What should you write?**
- A) I finished reading the book yesterday - Wrong: States a fact, does not persuade
- B) The book has 100 pages - Wrong: Just information, not persuasive
- **C) This book is so exciting that you will not be able to put it down!** - CORRECT: Uses exciting language to convince
- D) Books are made of paper - Wrong: Irrelevant fact, not persuasive

**Q11. Put the following sentences in order:**
1. Finally, I blew out the candles and made a wish.
2. First, my mom baked a chocolate cake.
3. Then, my friends came to my party.
4. After that, we played games and ate pizza.

- **A) 2, 3, 4, 1** - CORRECT: Logical order - cake baked, friends came, played/ate, then candles
- B) 1, 2, 3, 4 - Wrong: Candles come at the end, not first
- C) 3, 2, 1, 4 - Wrong: Cake must be baked before party
- D) 2, 4, 3, 1 - Wrong: Friends come before playing games

**Q12. Add the best topic sentence:**
- **A) Giraffes are interesting animals with special features.** - CORRECT: Introduces the topic of giraffe features
- B) Zebras have stripes - Wrong: Paragraph is about giraffes
- C) Elephants are the largest - Wrong: Not about giraffes
- D) All animals live in the jungle - Wrong: Not specific to the paragraph

**Q13. What does "butterflies in my stomach" mean?**
- A) Zara ate butterflies - Wrong: It is an idiom, not literal
- **B) Zara felt nervous and excited** - CORRECT: This idiom means feeling nervous
- C) Zara saw butterflies outside - Wrong: Not the meaning of the expression
- D) Zara had a stomach ache from food - Wrong: The feeling is from nervousness, not food

**Q14. What kind of person is Ahmed?**
- A) Lazy and careless - Wrong: He completes chores and homework
- **B) Responsible and hardworking** - CORRECT: He does chores, homework, and his parents are proud
- C) Angry and upset - Wrong: No evidence of negative emotions
- D) Scared and worried - Wrong: No evidence of fear

**Q15. Choose the sentence with the correct punctuation:**
- A) where is my pencil box - Wrong: Missing capital and question mark
- B) Where is my pencil box. - Wrong: Question needs question mark, not period
- **C) Where is my pencil box?** - CORRECT: Capital letter and question mark
- D) where Is My Pencil Box? - Wrong: Incorrect capitalization

**Q16. Which reason fits best with the opinion and example?**
- A) Pizza is round in shape - Wrong: Describes appearance, not why it is favorite
- B) Pizza is made in Italy - Wrong: Origin, not a personal reason
- **C) Pizza tastes delicious to me** - CORRECT: Connects opinion (favorite) to example (cheese and sauce)
- D) Pizza is sold in many stores - Wrong: Availability is not the reason

**Q17. Who is telling this story?**
- A) A librarian - Wrong: The narrator visits the library
- B) A dinosaur - Wrong: Narrator reads about dinosaurs
- **C) The reader/narrator who visits the library** - CORRECT: Uses "I" and describes visiting
- D) A book - Wrong: Books cannot narrate

**Q18. What is the story mostly about?**
- A) How to become a librarian - Wrong: Not instructional
- **B) Someone's love for the library and reading** - CORRECT: Describes enjoying the library
- C) How dinosaurs lived - Wrong: Just one book topic mentioned
- D) How to build a library - Wrong: Not mentioned

**Q19. You want to entertain someone with a joke about elephants. What should you write?**
- A) Elephants are the largest land animals - Wrong: This is informational, not entertaining
- **B) What do you call an elephant that does not matter? An irrelephant!** - CORRECT: This is a pun/joke
- C) Elephants live in Africa and Asia - Wrong: Factual, not entertaining
- D) Baby elephants are called calves - Wrong: Informational, not a joke

**Q20. Choose the best reply:**
*Amira: I am sorry I broke your crayon.*
- A) That is very bad of you! - Wrong: Too harsh, not kind
- **B) It is okay. Accidents happen.** - CORRECT: Kind, understanding response
- C) I do not want to talk to you - Wrong: Too harsh for an accident
- D) Crayons are expensive - Wrong: Makes the person feel worse

# DETAILED EXPLANATIONS FOR SELECTED QUESTIONS

## Common Question Types Explained:

### Topic Sentence Questions
The correct answer introduces the main idea of the paragraph and connects to all the supporting details that follow. Wrong answers are either:
- Too specific (only cover one detail)
- About a different topic
- Too general (do not match the specific content)

### Idiom Questions
Idioms have figurative meanings, not literal. Always choose the answer that makes sense figuratively:
- "Butterflies in stomach" = nervous
- "Piece of cake" = easy
- "Break the ice" = make people comfortable
- "Under the weather" = feeling sick
- "Once in a blue moon" = rarely

### Narrator Identification
Look for clues in the text:
- Physical descriptions (wings, tail, four legs)
- Actions (swimming, flying, meowing)
- Environment (hive, water, underground)
- How they describe humans ("my human")

### Sequencing Questions
Look for signal words:
- First, Then, Next, After that, Finally
- Logical order (cause before effect)
- Time sequence

### Persuasive Writing
The correct answer:
- Gives benefits/reasons
- Uses positive language
- Tries to convince
Wrong answers just state facts without persuading

### Rhyming Questions
The answer must:
- Sound the same at the end
- Make sense in context
- Fit the rhythm of the poem
